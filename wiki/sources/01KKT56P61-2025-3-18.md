# Cosmos-Reason1: Physical AI Reasoning via Long Chain-of-Thought

> NVIDIA's Cosmos-Reason1 introduces two multimodal LLMs (8B and 56B parameters) trained through a four-stage curriculum — vision pre-training, general SFT, Physical AI SFT, and RL post-training — to ground language model reasoning in physical world dynamics. The paper contributes structured ontologies for physical common sense and embodied reasoning, a scalable data curation pipeline using DeepSeek-R1 distillation, and demonstrates that RLVR can be extended to physical AI domains via self-supervised MCQ formats. A key finding: frontier models like GPT-4o and OpenAI o1 perform near random chance on arrow-of-time and object permanence tasks, revealing a deep and previously underappreciated gap in physical world understanding.

## Motivation

[[themes/reasoning_and_planning|Reasoning and Planning]] capabilities in LLMs have advanced dramatically through post-training and test-time scaling, particularly via [[themes/chain_of_thought|Chain-of-Thought]] processes. Models excel at coding and mathematics. But this progress has not translated to physical world understanding.

The grounding gap is stark: GPT-4o scores ~50% on arrow-of-time classification (random chance) and ~48% on object permanence. Standard multimodal benchmarks like MMMU do not expose this failure — models can pass them while being entirely blind to basic physical dynamics. No principled ontology existed to define what physical reasoning capabilities even are across diverse embodiments, and constructing verifiable RL rewards for open-ended physical reasoning had no clear solution.

## Approach

### Ontological Foundations

Two structured ontologies drive the entire system — from data curation to benchmark construction to RL reward design:

- **Physical common sense ontology:** Three major categories (Space, Time, Fundamental Physics) divided into 16 fine-grained subcategories
- **Embodied reasoning ontology:** Two-dimensional, mapping four key reasoning capabilities against five agent types (robot arms, humanoids, autonomous vehicles, humans, and others)

This creates a closed loop: capability definitions feed data curation, which feeds benchmarks, which feed RL rewards.

### Architecture

The backbone uses a hybrid Mamba-MLP-Transformer design (52 layers for 8B, 118 for 56B). [[themes/chain_of_thought|Chain-of-thought]] reasoning in long video contexts requires handling long sequences — Mamba's linear-time selective state space modeling addresses the quadratic complexity of full attention. However, selective state spaces alone are insufficient for capturing fine-grained detail, so sparse Transformer layers compensate. The vision encoder is InternViT-300M-V2.5, producing 1,024 tokens per frame, downsampled to 256 via PixelShuffle. Video input is sampled at up to 32 frames at ≤2fps, resized to 448×448.

### Four-Stage Training Curriculum

| Stage | What's Trained | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| Vision pre-training | Projector only (backbone + encoder frozen) | 130M samples |
| General SFT | Full fine-tuning | 6M image + 2M video samples |
| Physical AI SFT | Full fine-tuning on domain data | ~1.94M reasoning samples |
| Physical AI RL | GRPO with verifiable rewards | MCQ format throughout |

### Data Curation Pipeline

Physical AI SFT data is constructed through a model-in-the-loop process:
1. Human-annotated video captions are generated for source datasets (BridgeData V2, RoboVQA, AgiBot, HoloAssist, proprietary AV data)
2. Questions are constructed that *cannot* be answered from captions alone — requiring genuine visual reasoning
3. DeepSeek-R1 generates chain-of-thought reasoning traces using caption-structured prompts as distillation targets

This compresses visual content into text before reasoning — a practical workaround, but one that introduces hallucination risk and information loss.

### Extending RLVR to Physical AI

The key insight enabling [[themes/rl_for_llm_reasoning|RL for LLM Reasoning]] in physical domains: three self-supervised intuitive physics tasks naturally produce verifiable MCQ rewards with no manual labeling:

- **Spatial puzzles:** Shuffled 2×2 spatiotemporal patches — which arrangement is correct?
- **Arrow of time:** Is this video playing forward or reversed?
- **Object permanence:** Simulation-based occlusion/disappearance tasks

For embodied reasoning RL data, MCQ distractors require manual human verification to prevent reward hacking and ensure answer balance. This limits scale to 200–250 samples per data source.

GRPO is used without a separate critic model, with a combined accuracy reward (string matching) and format reward enforcing `<think>...</think><answer>...</answer>` structure.

## Results

### Benchmark Performance

Physical AI SFT improves backbone VLM performance by >10% on physical common sense and embodied reasoning benchmarks. Physical AI RL adds a further ~8% average gain.

**Physical common sense (56B):** 60.2% — marginally above OpenAI o1 (59.9%)
**Embodied reasoning (8B after RL):** 60.0% average (+12.8% over backbone), outperforming o1 (54.5%) and Qwen2.5-VL-72B (55.8%)
**Full benchmark average (8B after RL):** 67.1%, a +8.2% gain over SFT

### Intuitive Physics — Where Frontiers Fail

| Task | GPT-4o | OpenAI o1 | Cosmos-R1-8B (SFT) | Cosmos-R1-8B (RL) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arrow of time | 50.0% | ~50% | 57.0% | 56.7% |
| Spatial puzzle | 77.0% | ~77% | 85.2% | 87.0% |
| Object permanence | 48.0% | ~50% | 54.8% | 62.0% |

These results reveal that frontier models are genuinely blind to temporal irreversibility and object permanence — not just underperforming, but operating at chance level.

### Emergent RL Behavior

After RL post-training, the model learns to reject all provided MCQ options when a question is genuinely ambiguous — rather than forcing a choice. This behavior was not explicitly trained and suggests that verifiable reward signals can produce epistemic calibration as an emergent property.

## Limitations & Open Questions

### Active Failures

**Arrow-of-time reasoning remains intractable.** Even after SFT+RL, performance barely moves (57.0% → 56.7%). Temporal irreversibility may require fundamentally different representations than current VLM architectures provide. Trajectory: *unclear*.

**RoboFail affordance reasoning is stuck.** Complex real-world affordance questions show no improvement through either training stage, hovering around 62–66%. The paper attributes this to dataset characteristics but offers no resolution. Trajectory: *stable*.

**Quality of reasoning traces is unmeasured.** Only final answer accuracy is evaluated. The chain-of-thought supervision signal — sourced from DeepSeek-R1 operating on text captions, not video — may be systematically flawed in ways final accuracy metrics cannot reveal.

### Structural Bottlenecks

**The grounding gap** — LLMs cannot reliably connect linguistic physical knowledge to real-world dynamics. This is the foundational problem Cosmos-Reason1 addresses, and while it makes progress, the gap remains. Blocking: deployment of language models as reliable physical AI controllers. Horizon: *3–5 years*.

**RL reward scalability** — constructing verifiable rewards for open-ended physical reasoning is only solved by collapsing tasks to MCQ format. Free-form embodied decisions remain outside the reach of RLVR. Blocking: applying RL with verifiable rewards beyond narrow MCQ formats. Horizon: *1–2 years*.

**Embodied RL data requires human-in-the-loop.** Manual MCQ verification limits embodied reasoning RL data to 200–250 samples per source — a severe scalability ceiling. Blocking: large-scale RLVR post-training for embodied reasoning. Horizon: *1–2 years*.

**No closed-loop learning.** The model can reason about actions but cannot update from environmental consequences. "Learn from Interactions" is explicitly deferred to future work. Blocking: physical AI systems that improve through deployment experience. Horizon: *3–5 years*.

### Architectural Constraints

- Video input capped at 32 frames at ≤2fps — fast physical dynamics may be systematically missed
- Mamba selective state spaces require hybrid transformer layers to compensate for detail loss in long sequences
- Entire data pipeline depends on compressing visual content to text captions before DeepSeek-R1 reasoning — introducing hallucination risk at the supervision signal level

## Key Claims

1. Post-training and test-time scaling via long CoT has enabled strong LLM reasoning in coding and math, but models cannot ground this in physical world dynamics
2. Physical common sense organizes into a three-category (Space, Time, Fundamental Physics) hierarchical ontology with 16 fine-grained subcategories
3. Embodied reasoning maps across four capabilities and five agent types in a two-dimensional ontology
4. The hybrid Mamba-MLP-Transformer backbone handles long video sequences with linear-time complexity while using sparse Transformer layers for precision
5. Physical AI SFT improves backbone VLM performance by >10%; Physical AI RL adds ~8% further
6. GPT-4o and OpenAI o1 perform near random chance on arrow-of-time (~50%) and object permanence (~48%) despite strong standard benchmark performance
7. Arrow-of-time reasoning remains a challenge even after targeted SFT+RL, showing minimal improvement from RL

### Cross-Domain Implications
The MCQ-collapse solution for RLVR is a workaround, not a solution. The deeper question — how to construct verifiable rewards for open-ended physical interaction — remains open and likely requires either simulation-based verification, outcome-based evaluation from environment feedback, or a new class of learned reward models grounded in physical consequences rather than linguistic correctness.

The empirical demonstration that frontier models are blind to arrow-of-time and object permanence suggests that standard benchmark suites are systematically underestimating capability gaps in physical reasoning. This has implications for how [[themes/vision_language_action_models|VLA models]] are evaluated and deployed.
