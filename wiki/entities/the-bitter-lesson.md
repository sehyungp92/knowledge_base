## Overview

Richard Sutton's principle that general-purpose methods leveraging compute tend to outperform hand-crafted domain-specific approaches in the long run. Referenced here in the context of whether there is a non-obvious 'bitter lesson' path to hybrid models.

## Key Findings

1. Encoding researcher discoveries directly into AI systems makes it harder to develop the discovering process itself. (from "The Bitter Lesson")
2. Incorporating human knowledge into AI systems was ultimately counterproductive and a waste of research time once massive computation became available through Moore's Law. (from "The Bitter Lesson")
3. Search and learning are the two most important classes of techniques for utilizing massive amounts of computation in AI research. (from "The Bitter Lesson")
