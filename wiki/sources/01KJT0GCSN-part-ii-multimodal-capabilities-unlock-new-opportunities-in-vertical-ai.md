## Briefing

**Multimodal AI — spanning voice, vision, and video — is expanding vertical AI's addressable economy far beyond the text-based use cases that defined the first wave of generative AI adoption, with speech-native models and reasoning-focused agents representing the next step-function improvement. The article argues that as underlying models commoditize (as happened with text), durable differentiation will come from workflow integration depth, architectural ingenuity in stitching models together, and solving genuinely complex multi-modal reasoning tasks rather than single-modality point solutions. For investors and founders, the implication is that vertical AI is entering a second, more defensible phase where the breadth of the economy it can touch — including physical-world professions like medicine, construction, and manufacturing — is only beginning to be understood.**

### Key Takeaways
1. **The text-first era is giving way to multimodal expansion** — Most generative AI to date attacked text workflows (contracts, emails, copy), but the majority of real-world work involves audio, images, and video, meaning multimodal models unlock a substantially larger economic surface area.
2. **Speech-native models eliminate cascading architecture's core weaknesses** — Moving from transcribe→LLM→synthesize pipelines to end-to-end speech-to-speech (e.g., OpenAI Realtime API, Moshi) cuts latency below 500ms and preserves emotional/tonal context lost during text transcription.
3. **Inbound voice agents are already closing sales leads autonomously** — Purpose-built vertical voice agents can field after-hours calls, book appointments, and query CRM systems to quote prices without human intervention — a concrete, revenue-attributable deployment of conversational AI.
4. **Vision use cases follow a clear maturity gradient** — Data extraction from unstructured documents is most mature; visual inspection and 2D/3D design generation are emerging; video analytics (object tracking, NL search) are youngest but improving fastest.
5. **Reasoning-focused models (o1-class) are the unlock for truly autonomous agents** — Standard LLMs predict next tokens from patterns; inference-time chain-of-thought models "think before answering," enabling multi-step autonomous workflows that previous agents could not reliably execute.
6. **Agent defensibility comes from architecture, not raw model scale** — Unlike LLM training (where scale dominates), agentic performance is shaped by how models, guardrails, and feedback loops are stitched together — a more accessible moat for early-stage startups.
7. **Model commoditization is a feature, not a threat, for application builders** — The article explicitly predicts voice and vision models will commoditize as text models did, reducing input costs and making application-layer businesses more sustainable over time.
8. **Complexity ≠ value in vision applications** — Founders are warned not to conflate technical sophistication with customer ROI; a narrow, low-friction solution with clear workflow fit beats a technically impressive product that requires burdensome integrations.
9. **Omnichannel, multilingual, and real-time translation will become table stakes in voice** — Low latency and sentiment understanding are near-commoditized differentiators; future voice products will compete on orchestration across models, language support, and regulatory guardrails.
10. **Vertical AI is beginning to reach physical-world professions** — Construction (xBuild, Snaptrude), freight forwarding (Raft), medical documentation (Abridge), and manufacturing safety monitoring represent a qualitatively new category: AI embedded in field and physical workflows, not just knowledge work.
11. **Regulation is an explicit risk vector for outbound voice agents** — The article flags robocall regulation as a critical monitoring area, with compliant solutions restricted to opted-in leads and candidates.
12. **Novel business models (copilots, agents, AI-enabled services) are the next layer** — The article teases that product and pricing model innovation driven by multimodal AI will be addressed in a follow-up, signaling that the disruption extends beyond product capabilities into SaaS economics.

### The Transition from Text-Only to Multimodal AI

- The initial wave of commercial generative AI was dominated by text tasks — legal drafting, job descriptions, email composition, website copy — capturing the lowest-hanging fruit of enterprise workflows.
  - Demand for text solutions remains high, and AI's role in generating first drafts to redirect human effort toward complex functions is well established.
  - However, **a large share of daily work involves non-text modalities**: customer conversations, clinical discussions, image-heavy documents, construction blueprints, surveillance feeds.
- Multimodal models have meaningfully expanded the addressable economy for vertical AI, moving it from a segment of knowledge work to a much broader slice of how businesses actually operate.
  - The shift is framed not as incremental improvement but as a **category expansion** — industries previously inaccessible to software automation are now viable targets.
- Performance benchmarks in speech recognition, image processing, and voice generation are described as approaching or surpassing human-level capability in some models, a threshold that unlocks previously blocked use cases.

## Key Claims

1. Multimodal models have created opportunities for vertical AI to impact a much larger share of the economy than previously imagined by expanding beyond text-based tasks.
2. Speech, image processing, and voice generation performance in certain models is approaching or surpassing human capabilities.
3. Cascading architecture for conversational voice introduces additional latency and loses non-textual context such as emotion and sentiment during transcription.
4. Speech-native models have substantially lower latency (under 500 milliseconds) than previous cascading architecture models.
5. Speech-native models can capture tone, sentiment, and emotion from users, making conversations feel more natural.
6. A step function change in the speed and quality of conversational voice applications is anticipated over the next few years as more are built on speech-native models.
7. Voice agents for inbound sales can close leads without requiring interactions or interventions from a sales representative.
8. Modern voice agents can provide correct answers regardless of how customers phrase their questions, unlike traditional IVR which required specific phrasing.
9. Google's Gemini 1.5 Pro can understand both image and video input and retain contextual understanding up to one million input tokens.
10. Vision and similar multimodal models are expected to continue to improve in performance and decrease in cost.

## Capabilities

- Speech-native models (GPT-4o Realtime API, Moshi) enable direct speech-to-speech conversations with sub-500ms latency and capture of tone, sentiment, and emotion without transcription intermediary
- End-to-end conversational voice agents can handle inbound sales calls autonomously — fielding inquiries, booking appointments, querying pricing systems, and closing leads without human intervention
- Medical transcription AI can generate structured clinical notes from doctor-patient conversations and identify appropriate follow-ups including prescriptions, specialist referrals, and billing codes
- Vision AI can assess physical damage from photographs — analysing roof damage and construction blueprints to generate scope-of-work reports aligned with local building codes
- AI can generate detailed 3D architectural designs of buildings, automating the repetitive placement work typically performed by structural engineers
- Video understanding models can perform object tracking, classification, and natural language search over video feeds, enabling production safety-monitoring applications in manufacturing and industrial settings
- Gemini 1.5 Pro processes and retains contextual understanding across image and video inputs up to one million tokens
- AI negotiation agents can autonomously conduct simultaneous multi-party commercial negotiations — mapping value functions and optimising deal terms across supply chain suppliers
- AI security triage agents can autonomously gather evidence from multiple disparate systems, research malicious behaviours, and summarise and grade the severity of cybersecurity alerts
- o1-style reasoning models apply chain-of-thought inference-time computation to plan, assess, and solve complex reasoning tasks — outperforming standard next-token-prediction LLMs on hard problems

## Limitations

- Cascading voice architecture (STT → LLM → TTS) loses non-textual emotional and sentiment context from users, degrading response quality and conversational naturalness
- Cascading voice architecture introduces compounding latency from three sequential model inference steps, preventing real-time conversational responsiveness
- AI agents fail on complex multi-step workflows due to compounding errors across reasoning steps — requiring task scoping and constraint to operate reliably
- Video generation and understanding models are the least mature vision modality, limiting their applicability in production vertical AI deployments
- Regulatory constraints on unsolicited automated outbound calls materially limit the addressable market for AI voice agents in sales and recruiting
- Implicit: AI agents deployed in complex workflows cannot operate without constrained scope and architectural guardrails — unconstrained autonomous operation across open-ended tasks is not yet possible
- Implicit: AI-driven design automation requiring integration with incumbent industry systems (e.g., Revit in AEC) faces adoption-killing complexity — deep integrations with critical legacy SoRs block early ROI
- Implicit: Until very recently, the vast majority of real-world economic activity — anything requiring audio, voice, or visual reasoning — was inaccessible to AI automation, limiting AI's economic impact to narrow text tasks
- Implicit: o1-style reasoning models are in early, unvalidated territory — their performance on truly complex, open-ended tasks has not been established
- Implicit: Standard LLMs are fundamentally incapable of deliberate planning — they are stateless next-token predictors without inference-time reasoning, making them structurally unsuited for complex agent tasks
- Implicit: Conversational voice AI prior to speech-native models was not capable of emotion- or sentiment-aware responses — a systemic limitation invisible to users building on cascading architectures
- Implicit: Agent performance in complex workflows is not a scaling-law problem — orchestration quality, model selection, and guardrail design dominate outcomes, meaning there is no clear path to improvement via simply scaling compute

## Bottlenecks

- Cascading voice architecture (STT→LLM→TTS) creates compounding latency and discards emotional/sentiment context, blocking natural low-latency conversational voice applications
- Agent compounding errors in multi-step reasoning block reliable complex workflow automation — agents can only be safely deployed on constrained, narrow-scope tasks
- Video generation and understanding model immaturity blocks video-based vertical AI applications — particularly in robotics perception and advanced video analytics use cases
- Deep integration requirements with critical legacy industry systems (e.g., Revit, ERPs) impose high upfront cost and low initial ROI, blocking AI design and workflow tools from achieving early adoption in established verticals
- Regulatory constraints on automated outbound calls (robocall laws) block AI voice agents from fully automating outbound sales and recruiting workflows

## Breakthroughs

- Speech-native end-to-end models (OpenAI Realtime API / GPT-4o, Moshi) eliminate the cascading voice architecture, achieving sub-500ms conversational latency and full preservation of emotional/sentiment context
- o1-style inference-time chain-of-thought reasoning establishes a new paradigm where models 'think before answering' — separating the planning and answering steps in a way standard autoregressive LLMs cannot
