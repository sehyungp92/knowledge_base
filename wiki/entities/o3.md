## Overview

OpenAI's dedicated reasoning model, priced at $2.00 input / $8.00 output, used for hard search and reasoning tasks.

## Capabilities

- One-shot resolution of complex nested dependency conflicts across large codebases, succeeding where frontier models (o3, Claude Opus 4) failed entirely (maturity: narrow_production)
- Test-time compute scaling (o1, o3) unlocks capabilities that larger base models cannot achieve by allowing models to 'think longer' at inference (maturity: narrow_production)
- Visual reasoning integrated into chain of thought: o3 and o4-mini integrate images directly into their thinking process — including dynamically manipulating images (rotating, zooming, transforming) mi (maturity: narrow_production)
- SWE-Terminal-Bench leading score of 37.5%, outperforming o3 (30.2%), GPT-4.1 (30.3%), Claude 4 Sonnet (35.5%), and Gemini 2.5 Pro (25.3%) on terminal-based agentic software engineering (maturity: narrow_production)
- AIME24 91.0% (Avg@32) on olympiad-level mathematics, competitive with o3 (90.3%) and ahead of Gemini 2.5 Pro (88.7%); MATH 500 at 98.2% (maturity: narrow_production)

## Known Limitations

- Rate limits for o3 and o4-mini remain unchanged from predecessor models — high-volume users face the same throughput ceiling despite the capability step-change (severity: minor, trajectory: stable)
- HLE (Humanity's Last Exam) score of 14.4% reveals a significant performance cliff on genuinely hard, novel cross-domain reasoning — 6-10 point gap behind Gemini 2.5 Pro (21.1%), o3 (20.0%), and Grok4  (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
- o3-pro — the most compute-intensive model variant — launches without full tool support, temporarily making the 'most capable' model less capable than o3 for agentic tasks (severity: minor, trajectory: improving)
- All frontier LRMs (o3-mini, DeepSeek-R1, Claude-3.7-Sonnet Thinking) experience complete accuracy collapse to zero beyond a model-specific complexity threshold — no amount of additional inference comp (severity: blocking, trajectory: unclear)
- Models remain unreliable in worst-case health scenarios: o3's worst-at-16 score drops by one-third relative to its average score, meaning that across many independent samples there are still frequent  (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
