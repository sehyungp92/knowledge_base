## Key Findings

1. An inflection is a specific new change event—not a trend—that creates a turning point in the capacity to empower people in a new and profound way. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
2. Twitter's initial concept was described by Evan Williams as 140-character status updates with no roadmap and no revenue model. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
3. A startup's insight is the creative 'so what' derived from an inflection—it must be non-consensus and provide radical empowerment. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
4. Pattern-breaking startups must force a choice rather than invite a comparison, because no rational customer should prefer an 80%-likely-to-fail startup over an incumbent unless the startup's offer can (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
5. Justin.TV pivoted from broadcasting individual life in real time to enabling gamers to stream, which became Twitch. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
6. Startups create value by changing the subject (pattern breaking), not by compounding persistent advantages like established corporations. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
7. Twitter was conceived as a side project at Odio, with the working name 'twttr' or 'voicemail 2.0', limited to 140 characters (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
8. Brian Chesky funded Airbnb early by selling themed cereal boxes (Obama O's and Captain McCain Crunch) (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
9. The speaker's first two entrepreneur investments were Evan Williams and Kevin Rose, which he attributes primarily to luck (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
10. The early seed fund thesis was '500K is the new $5M', catching the Lean Startup movement at the exact time there was a funding gap for that type of company. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
11. Twitter blew up at South by Southwest conference, after which it became a hot deal in Silicon Valley. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
12. Lyft used the pink mustache to overcome customer resistance to getting in a stranger's car, functioning as a narrative device to lower adoption barriers. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
13. Kevin Rose started Digg for $1,500 over a weekend and primarily needed money for servers rather than marketing. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
14. The iPhone 4S's embedded GPS chip was the specific inflection that enabled ride-sharing companies like Lyft to exist. (from "The Legendary Mike Maples: What Makes Startups Win & The Future of Venture Capital")
