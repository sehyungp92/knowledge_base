## Overview

OpenAI's reasoning system trained with large-scale RL on long chain-of-thought traces. Demonstrates inference scaling laws and performs online search at generation time. The deployed o1 preview is a significantly scaled-down version of the full system.

## Key Findings

1. The validation dataset for RFT training had no overlap in correct genes with the training dataset, ensuring the model must generalize rather than memorize. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
2. Reinforcement fine-tuning is the same technique OpenAI uses internally to train frontier models like GPT-4o and the o1 series. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
3. RFT graders score model outputs on a continuous scale from 0 to 1 and can assign partial credit. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
4. RFT o1 mini achieved 31% top-at-1 accuracy on the rare genetic disease gene identification task, outperforming both base o1 mini (17%) and o1 (25%). (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
5. Reinforcement fine-tuning works by grading the model's final answers and using reinforcement learning to reinforce lines of thinking that led to correct answers while disincentivizing those that led t (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
6. The correct answer label in RFT training data is not shown to the model during training, but is used internally to grade the model's outputs. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
7. The rare disease dataset includes lists of both present symptoms and absent symptoms, with absent symptoms helping to rule out candidate genes. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
8. The training dataset for the rare disease gene identification task consisted of approximately 1,100 examples extracted from case reports. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
9. RFT training data is formatted as JSONL files where each line contains a training example. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
10. o1 was primarily a technology demonstration rather than a polished or broadly useful product, being mostly good at solving puzzles. (from "How GPT-5 Thinks — OpenAI VP of Research Jerry Tworek")
11. Reinforcement fine-tuning teaches models to learn to reason in entirely new ways over custom domains, rather than mimicking input text as in supervised fine-tuning. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
12. o1 performance consistently improves with both more RL training compute and more test-time thinking compute (from "Reverse engineering OpenAI’s o1")
13. The dataset for rare disease gene identification was assembled collaboratively between Berkeley Lab, Charité Hospital in Germany, Peter Robinson's lab, and the Monarch Initiative. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")
14. O1 is OpenAI's first major foray into general inference-time compute and reasoning. (from "OpenAI's Noam Brown, Ilge Akkaya and Hunter Lightman on o1 and Teaching LLMs to Reason Better")
15. o1 mini is a smaller, faster, and cheaper model than o1, yet RFT allows it to exceed o1's performance on domain-specific tasks. (from "Reinforcement Fine-Tuning—12 Days of OpenAI: Day 2")

## Capabilities

- Test-time compute scaling (o1, o3) unlocks capabilities that larger base models cannot achieve by allowing models to 'think longer' at inference (maturity: narrow_production)
- o3 sets new SOTA on SWE-bench Verified without a custom model-specific scaffold, and on Codeforces and MMMU, making 20% fewer major errors than o1 on difficult real-world tasks across programming, bus (maturity: narrow_production)
- o3 and o4-mini simultaneously outperform and cost less than predecessor models o1 and o3-mini respectively, achieving a strictly improving cost-performance frontier (maturity: broad_production)
- Multimodal LLMs can reason about physical common sense (space, time, fundamental physics) from video input using long chain-of-thought, achieving scores competitive with or exceeding OpenAI o1 on stru (maturity: demo)

## Known Limitations

- State-of-the-art frontier models without TTT (Claude 3.5 Sonnet: 21%, GPT-4o: 9%, o1 preview: 21%, DeepSeek R1: 20.5%) show extremely poor ARC performance, confirming that scale and RLHF alone do not  (severity: significant, trajectory: stable)
- Current SOTA multimodal models (GPT-4o, Gemini 2.0 Flash, OpenAI o1) perform at or near random chance on arrow-of-time and object permanence tasks, despite strong performance on standard benchmarks li (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
