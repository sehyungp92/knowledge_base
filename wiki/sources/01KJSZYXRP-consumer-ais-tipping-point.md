## Briefing

**We are entering a new wave of "consumer earthquakes" driven by AI-native products, and the defining open question is no longer whether AI can generate viral adoption — it can — but whether those moments convert into defensible, gross-margin-positive businesses.** Three structural forces (collapsing model costs, multimodal/real-time capabilities, and agentic reasoning) are converging to create five high-potential consumer categories where AI democratizes services once gatekept by professional expertise or wealth.

### Key Takeaways
1. **Token costs collapsed 85–95% in two years** — Since GPT-4's release in March 2023, per-token costs dropped dramatically, and OpenAI's API leadership expects the decline to continue, directly unlocking consumer business model viability.
2. **Consumer companies are the primary beneficiaries of model deflation** — B2B AI firms absorb costs via high ACVs; consumer firms with low per-interaction value have historically been margin-squeezed, making further cost decline structurally transformative.
3. **Viral AI adoption is already proven; defensibility is the unsolved problem** — Perplexity, Anthropic, and DeepL are compounding organically, but sustainable moats in consumer AI remain an open question the entire industry is racing to answer.
4. **Long-term memory turns AI products into compounding personalization engines** — Zep AI and Zetta enable agents that accumulate user context over time, making the product more tailored with each interaction — a potentially powerful retention and switching-cost mechanism.
5. **Real-time AI unlocks replacement of high-friction manual tasks** — Near-zero-latency APIs (OpenAI Realtime) enable voice AI that can schedule doctors appointments in real time, opening a new surface area for automation.
6. **AI is doing for services what mobile did for logistics** — Just as Uber and DoorDash democratized transportation and food delivery via smartphone-scale liquidity, AI will democratize access to professional services (financial advice, fitness coaching, travel planning) at consumer price points.
7. **Text-to-app platforms are collapsing the barrier to software creation** — Tools like Bolt, Create.xyz, and Lovable allow non-technical users to build functional apps in minutes, threatening to commoditize basic software development while expanding the creator economy.
8. **27 million Americans already identify as creators** — The prosumer market is already large and AI is poised to expand it further by lowering barriers to professional-grade content production (film, music, fashion, software).
9. **Product discovery is a structurally broken market ripe for disruption** — Reliance on manual filters and poorly tagged metadata creates a gap that AI-powered natural language and multimodal search (virtual try-on, conversational queries) is beginning to close.
10. **AI agents are moving from search to action** — OpenAI's Operator and Perplexity's assistant product mark a shift from AI answering questions to AI completing tasks, a transition with significant SaaS displacement implications.
11. **Consumer AI is inherently a democratization story** — Every major category (professional services, creation tools, product discovery, software development) follows the same logic: make something previously expensive, slow, or expertise-gated available to anyone.

---

### The "Consumer Earthquake" Framework

- **Consumer earthquakes share two universal traits**, regardless of era: jaw-dropping product experiences that are better, cheaper, easier, and more beloved than alternatives, and viral adoption that achieves a positive resting growth rate — organic growth without paid marketing.
  - Pinterest and Airbnb are cited as exemplars: both succeeded not in a vacuum but at the intersection of technological shifts and behavioral change, creating billion-dollar companies at unprecedented speed.
- **AI functions simultaneously as a seismic product force and a distribution vector** — unlike prior technology waves where infrastructure and distribution were separate layers, AI improvements directly improve product quality, which drives organic growth, which reduces CAC.
  - This dual role is a structural advantage not seen in prior consumer tech waves.
- **The phase shift question**: viral consumer moments are confirmed as achievable; the unresolved challenge is translating them into long-term defensible businesses with durable margins.
  - This framing implies that investors and founders should stress-test not just growth mechanics but moat formation.

---

### Why Model Cost Deflation Is a Consumer-Specific Structural Unlock

- **85–95% cost reduction in two years** on GPT-4 tokens is not a marginal improvement — it crosses thresholds that change which business models are viable at all.
  - B2B AI companies (ACVs in the tens/hundreds of thousands) were insulated from high model costs; consumer AI companies with $5–$20/month subscription economics were structurally unprofitable at 2023 model prices.
- **The path to gross margin positivity** for consumer AI now runs through continued cost deflation, enabling ad-supported, subscription, and usage-based models to work at consumer price points.
  - This is the mechanism by which model commoditization (bad for model providers) is net positive for the consumer application layer.
- **Foundation model competition among OpenAI, Anthropic, Grok, and others** is the structural driver — competitive dynamics ensure continued price pressure regardless of individual provider strategy.

### Multimodal and Real-Time Capabilities as New Product Surface Area

- **Step-change improvements across modalities** — speech recognition and voice generation (OpenAI, Rev, ElevenLabs), image and video generation (Black Forest Labs, RunwayML, Google Veo) are not incremental; they enable qualitatively new product

## Key Claims

1. The cost of a token on GPT-4 decreased by 85–95% between March 2023 and the time of writing.
2. OpenAI's Head of API product expects the trend of declining token costs to continue.
3. Consumer AI companies stand to benefit more from declining foundation model costs than B2B AI companies, because B2B companies have high ACVs that absorb model costs more easily.
4. Lower model costs will enable consumer companies with ad-supported, subscription, or usage-based pricing to become gross margin positive over time.
5. Multimodal foundation models have demonstrated step-change performance improvements in speech recognition, voice generation, image, and video.
6. Real-time models such as OpenAI's Realtime API are significantly reducing the latency of multimodal models.
7. Near-real-time foundation models will allow consumer companies to replace highly manual tasks such as scheduling doctor appointments via voice AI.
8. OpenAI's o3 has demonstrated significantly improved performance on complex reasoning tasks.
9. As foundation model reasoning improves, application-layer AI agents will be able to handle increasingly complex tasks.
10. OpenAI launched Operator, an AI agent that autonomously performs web-based tasks such as ordering groceries.

## Capabilities

- LLM API token costs have declined 85–95% since GPT-4's launch in March 2023, making B2C AI economics increasingly viable for low-ACV consumer products
- Multimodal foundation models demonstrate step-change performance improvements in speech recognition, voice generation, and image/video generation across multiple providers
- Real-time and near-real-time multimodal APIs (e.g., OpenAI Realtime API) enable latency-sensitive consumer voice applications such as automated appointment scheduling
- AI agents can autonomously perform web-based consumer tasks such as ordering groceries, making reservations, and setting reminders without human intervention
- Long-term memory infrastructure for AI agents (Zep AI, Zetta) enables hyper-personalized experiences that adapt to individual user behavior and preferences across sessions
- o3 demonstrates significantly improved performance on complex reasoning tasks, enabling automation of nuanced consumer interactions previously requiring human professionals
- Text-to-app platforms (Create.xyz, Bolt, Lovable) enable non-technical consumers to build functional software applications in minutes from natural language descriptions
- AI-powered natural language product search enables context-aware, personalized consumer shopping via conversational and text interfaces
- AI life-admin assistants autonomously manage calendars, appointments, reservations, and prescription refills on behalf of consumers
- Generative AI enables virtual fashion try-on via AI-generated user lookalikes, replacing static product model images with user-specific previews
- Generative AI enables fashion designers and brands to create custom clothing designs without traditional design tooling expertise
- Video and audio foundation models (Moonvalley, Runway, Sora) combined with AI editing tools (Kaiber) reduce the expertise, time, and capital required for professional video and film production

## Limitations

- Consumer AI businesses are not yet gross margin positive — current model costs relative to low B2C transaction values produce structurally negative unit economics for most consumer AI products
- AI replacement of professional service roles (financial advisors, travel agents, fitness instructors) remains a future aspiration rather than a present capability
- Long-term defensibility of consumer AI businesses is unproven — viral adoption moments do not automatically translate into sustainable competitive moats
- AI e-commerce product discovery remains cumbersome — manual filters, poorly tagged metadata, and absence of true semantic personalization persist as the dominant paradigm
- No proven advertising model exists for consumer AI products — ad-supported monetization of dialogue-format AI is discussed aspirationally but no working format is operational
- AI-enabled consumer product creation (fashion, interior design, digital content) is nascent — no dominant platforms, consumer behaviors, or end-to-end workflows are yet established
- Virtual fashion try-on is limited to AI-generated user lookalikes — true garment fit, fabric drape, and sizing simulation are absent from current consumer solutions
- AI generative gaming and social experiences lack established social layers — current products begin with AI-only interactivity, with real human social features deferred to future phases

## Bottlenecks

- Consumer AI B2C unit economics are structurally challenged — low per-interaction revenue relative to foundation model API costs prevents most consumer AI products from reaching gross margin positivity
- Absence of proven consumer AI monetization formats — no working ad model, established subscription benchmarks, or usage-based pricing norms exist for dialogue-format AI at consumer scale

## Breakthroughs

- LLM API token costs dropped 85–95% in roughly two years since GPT-4's launch, fundamentally changing the unit economics of B2C AI product development
- o3's step-change in complex reasoning enables AI agents to automate nuanced multi-step consumer experiences that previously required human professional judgment
