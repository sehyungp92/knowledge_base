# Language Models that Think, Chat Better
2025-09-24 · paper · Adithya Bhaskar, Xi Ye, Danqi Chen
https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.20357v1

---

### Motivation & Prior Limitations
- RLVR (reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards) has become the dominant method for training reasoning models, but it fails to generalize to open-ended, general-purpose tasks that constitute the majority of real-world LM use.
  - RLVR has been applied almost exclusively to math, code, STEM problems, and deterministic puzzles — domains where rule-based reward signals are available.
  - Open-source reasoning models trained via math-focused RLVR (e.g., General Reasoner, SimpleRL-Zoo, OpenThinker2-7B) score between 4.9 and 22.2 on WildBench, lagging far behind standard instruction-tuned models (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct at 22.2), despite having learned to "think."
  - The skills acquired from RL in verifiable domains — such as systematic chain-of-thought and iterative self-correction — do not transfer naturally to tasks like drafting essay outlines, writing emails, or making meal plans, where humans rely on reasoning routinely.
- Standard RLHF produces aligned chat models but does not encourage explicit reasoning or long chain-of-thought before generating responses, leaving the "thinking" capability untapped for general tasks.

---

### Proposed Approach
- The paper introduces **RL with Model-rewarded Thinking (RLMT)**, which combines the long chain-of-thought mechanism from RLVR with the preference-based reward model from RLHF, enabling online RL over open-ended, general-purpose prompts.
  - Unlike RLVR, which requires rule-based verifiers tied to ground-truth answers, RLMT uses a learned reward model (Skywork-v1-Llama-3.1-8B-v0.2) trained on human preference data to score responses — making it applicable to any domain.
  - Unlike standard RLHF, the model is required to produce a reasoning trace `z` before the final response `y`, so the optimization objective is `max_θ E_{x~X} E_{(y,z)~π_θ(·|x)} r(y, x)` — combining RLHF's reward signal with RLVR's explicit reasoning structure.
  - The paper evaluates three optimization algorithms — on-policy DPO, PPO, and GRPO — across two model families (Llama-3.1-8B and Qwen-2.5-7B, both base and instruct), producing 40 training runs for rigorous comparison.
- Two initialization strategies are studied: (1) **warm-start SFT**, where models are first fine-tuned on 6K thinking traces generated by Gemini 2.5 Flash (simulated since Gemini's CoT is not accessible), followed by RL; and (2) **zero training**, where RLMT is applied directly to base models without any SFT stage, analogous to R1-Zero training.
  - The prompt mixture for RL is drawn from 7.5K real-world conversational prompts from the WildChat-IF subset of Tulu-3, prioritizing diverse, realistic user queries rather than math or jailbreak-heavy distributions.
  - Ablations confirm that prompt mixture composition and reward model quality are both critical: WildChat-IF outperforms UltraFeedback (prompts too simple) and random Tulu-3 (too many math/jailbreak prompts), and the stronger Skywork-V2 reward model substantially outperforms the weaker ArmoRM.

---

### Results & Capabilities
- RLMT consistently outperforms standard RLHF baselines (trained under identical settings without thinking) across all four backbone configurations, all three RL algorithms, and all seven evaluation benchmarks.
  - Average gains of 3–7 points on three primary chat benchmarks (AlpacaEval2, WildBench, ArenaHardV2) and 1–3 points on secondary tasks including creative writing (CreativeWritingV3) and general knowledge (PopQA, MMLU-Redux).
  - GRPO outperforms PPO by ~1–3 points and DPO by ~5 points on average; however, RLMT's advantage over RLHF holds across all three algorithms.
- The best model, **Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct-RLMT (GRPO)**, scores 58.7 on AlpacaEval2, 50.4 on WildBench, and 22.9 on ArenaHardV2, outperforming Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct and Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct (models 10× larger) by 9–22 points, and surpassing GPT-4o on chat and creative writing.
  - It also outperforms Claude-3.7-Sonnet (Thinking) on AlpacaEval2 and WildBench, though it is weaker on ArenaHardV2, likely due to that benchmark's higher concentration of math and coding problems.
- In zero training (no SFT), RLMT with GRPO on Llama-3.1-8B base achieves an average chat score of 15.6, exceeding Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (10.1) despite the instruct model being post-trained on a complex multi-stage pipeline with 25M+ examples including rejection sampling and iterative preference optimization; Qwen-2.5-7B-RLMT-Zero achieves 29.0, exceeding Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct (23.1).
  - DPO and PPO are largely ineffective for zero training, confirming that GRPO is a necessary ingredient for this setting.
- Qualitative and quantitative analysis of reasoning traces reveals that RL training shifts Llama-3.1-8B from **linear, checklist-style planning** (SFT stage) to richer behaviors: constraint enumeration, theme grouping, iterative refinement, trade-off weighing, and cross-referencing across sections — strategies characteristic of skilled writers.
  - CoT length and response length both increase monotonically over training steps, replicating the "thinking more over time" dynamic observed in DeepSeek-R1-Zero.
- An unexpected finding is that while Llama-3.1 underperforms Qwen-2.5 before RL training, the ordering reverses after RLMT, suggesting RLMT unlocks latent capabilities that were present in pretraining but insufficiently surfaced by Qwen-2.5's more heavily benchmark-targeted post-training.
- RLMT models trained on chat prompts also outperform math-RLVR thinking models on chat by 10–25 points, and this gap persists even after applying additional RL to the math-trained models.

## Key Claims

1. RLVR (reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards) leads to limited generalization for open-ended tasks such as writing outline essays or making meal plans
2. Open-source reasoning models trained via math-focused RLVR lag behind standard instruction-tuned models on WildBench, a general-purpose chat benchmark
3. Skills acquired from RL training in verifiable domains do not naturally transfer to general chat tasks
4. RLMT (RL with Model-rewarded Thinking) trains language models to generate long chain-of-thought reasoning before final answers using online RL algorithms such as GRPO, evaluated against a preference-b
5. An 8B RLMT model surpasses GPT-4o on chat and creative writing benchmarks
6. Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct-RLMT outperforms Claude-3.7-Sonnet (thinking) on AlpacaEval2 and WildBench, though it performs worse on ArenaHardV2 likely due to that benchmark's high proportion of math and cod
7. RLMT can be applied directly to base models without an SFT stage, analogous to R1-Zero training
8. With only 7K prompts, Llama-3.1-8B base trained with RLMT outperforms Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct post-trained with a complex multi-stage pipeline using 25M+ examples
9. RLMT thinking models outperform non-thinking RLHF counterparts by 1.5–4 points on average across all benchmarks, with the gap being largest on chat (3–8 points on WildBench and AlpacaEval2)
10. GRPO achieves the best overall performance among RL algorithms (DPO, PPO, GRPO), outperforming PPO by about 1–3 points and DPO by about 5 points on average

## Capabilities

- RLMT (RL with Model-rewarded Thinking) enables long chain-of-thought training via online RL for open-ended, non-verifiable tasks — chat, creative writing, general knowledge — using preference-based reward models instead of rule-based verifiers
- An 8B parameter model trained with RLMT surpasses GPT-4o on chat and creative writing benchmarks and rivals Claude-3.7-Sonnet (Thinking), outperforming open-source models 10x its size by 9–22 points
- RLMT applied directly to base models (Zero training) with only 7K prompts produces models that outperform instruction-tuned models trained on 25M+ examples through complex multi-stage pipelines
- Long CoT RL training induces qualitative shifts in reasoning strategy for open-ended tasks: models shift from linear checklist planning to iterative refinement with constraint enumeration, theme grouping, and cross-section revision

## Limitations

- RLVR models trained on verifiable domains (math, code) do not generalize to open-ended chat tasks — reasoning skills from math-focused RL are domain-specific and actively underperform standard instruction-tuned models on general chat
- RLMT performance is critically dependent on reward model quality — a weak reward model (ArmoRM) causes large performance drops, especially on non-chat benchmarks when thinking is enabled, suggesting long CoT amplifies reward signal errors
- RLMT gains are highly sensitive to the composition of the RL prompt mixture — training on non-chat prompts (math, jailbreak) significantly degrades chat performance; there is no principled methodology for constructing optimal distributions
- Whether RLMT improvements arise from amplifying pre-existing latent capabilities or from learning genuinely new traits is unknown — the mechanism of improvement is not understood, limiting systematic pipeline design
- RLMT-trained 8B model underperforms Claude-3.7-Sonnet on ArenaHardV2, which has high proportions of math and coding — gains on open-ended chat quality do not transfer proportionally to technical reasoning benchmarks
- DPO and PPO are ineffective for zero-shot base model training with long CoT — only GRPO produces meaningful improvements when skipping the SFT warm-start stage, constraining Zero training to a single algorithm
- RLMT warm-start SFT depends on access to a frontier thinking model (Gemini 2.5 Flash, GPT-4.1-mini) for generating training traces — the pipeline is not self-contained and cannot be applied without proprietary teacher models
- Instruction following (IFBench) shows minimal improvement from RLMT relative to chat benchmarks — extended thinking does not proportionally improve precise constraint adherence
- CoT format, hyperparameters, and prompt mixture construction were not extensively optimized — the reported results are a lower bound of RLMT's potential, with no exploration of optimal thinking trace structure

## Bottlenecks

- Reward model quality is a critical dependency for model-rewarded RL training — weak reward models cause performance degradation that is amplified by long CoT, creating a circular dependency where RLMT requires strong preference models that are themselves expensive to produce
- No principled methodology exists for constructing optimal RL prompt mixtures for general-purpose training — empirical search over prompt distributions is required, blocking systematic deployment of RLMT across new task types

## Breakthroughs

- RLMT demonstrates that the RLVR paradigm — long CoT reasoning plus online RL — is effective for open-ended non-verifiable tasks when preference-based reward models substitute for rule-based verifiers, collapsing the distinction between reasoning models and chat models
- 7K prompts + RLMT base model training outperforms a complex multi-stage pipeline with 25M+ examples — extreme data efficiency achieved by combining long CoT with online RL undermines the assumption that competitive post-training requires massive curated datasets
