# A Deep Dive into the Future of Voice in AI

**Authors:** 
**Published:** 2024-11-19 00:00:00
**Type:** video

## Analysis

How LiveKit Works with ChatGPT Voice

- In terms of how LiveKit works with ChatGPT voice and the whole workflow:
	- First there is a LiveKit SDK that sits on the device responsible for being able to access the camera and the microphone of that device\. When the user says something, it captures that speech and sends it over LiveKit's network\. 
	- There is also an edge network, with servers all around the world that all communicate with one another and form a mesh network fabric in the backend, where whenever a user says something on their phone, the on device LiveKit SDK hits a network, transmits their audio through the network to an agent on the backend, which is serves the same role as an application server in a web application\. 
		- In the case of ChatGPT voice, OpenAI builds an agent using this framework on the backend that is sitting there as a receiver, getting that audio data coming from the user's device over the network onto the server and the agent receives that audio\. 
	- With the traditional voice mode before advanced voice, it was going through a speech to text step, converting the audio from the user into text before sending it to the LLM\. And as the tokens stream out of the LLM, they are getting converted back into speech, sent back over LiveKit's network to the client device where they are received by that LiveKit SDK on the client device, and played out through the phone or the desktop computer or website\. 
	- In the case of advanced voice, that speech goes from the client over the network to the agent that then sent directly into through the real time API to send it directly to GPT\-4o as audio\. GPT\-4o is jointly trained and has a joint embedding between text and speech tokens, meaning that inference is done directly on the audio embeddings\. And then the speech generated by GPT\-4o comes back out through the network to the device and played out\. 
- If the foundational model companies like OpenAI and Anthropic are building the brain, then arguably LiveKit is building a nervous system, the infrastructure that is able to take information from those senses, transport them to that brain and then take the result of that brain and transport it back out\.
	- Humans are very much tool builders, building tools that amplify our abilities\. And in that context, AGI is simply tool builders building tool builders in the form of a synthetic human being\. 
	- And so if there is a computer that is effectively like a human brain, responding to you and acting like a human, the way you interface with that computer is probably not with a keyboard and a mouse, but the way that we communicate with one another, i\.e\., using our eyes, ears and mouths, where the computer version of the eyes is a camera, the ears are microphones, the mouth is a speaker\. 

Future of Work and AI Interfaces

- The nature of work is going to change drastically\. In general, creative tools are going to become more voice based, more interactive, more multimodal\. 
	- Just as Tony Stark interacts with Jarvis in different ways, that's definitely going to happen for creative tools, where a lot of operations will happen on the actual assets, but you'll be the orchestrator of it\. 
- There has been a debate about copilots versus agents, but it may very well be a hybrid of both, just like how some people just completely own some part of your business and drive it forward autonomously, and then in other contexts, you are building something with someone interactively\. 
	- The main difference ultimately will be that you are likely to do a lot of this with AI and maybe not as much with humans\. 
- Also, just as we still use text to interact with each other, read text on the Internet, and type into boxes, there are going to be places that continue to use text\. 
	- Text, voice, computer vision, as human beings are visual creatures are all going to be an important part of the future\. 
	- Text has its place\. For instance, to use in a restaurant, if an agent knows the users’ preferences then a voice interface will be perfect\. But if it's, say, a new restaurant and the AI doesn't know exactly what the user knows want, you don't want it to read the entire menu to you, that is not a good UX\. 
- The future is definitely a hybrid, where voice makes sense in certain contexts, or in certain cases it will be voice plus text, or plus some generated on the fly UI where you can just tap a few buttons\.
	- Obvious examples where voice is a natural modality include hands free interfaces, e\.g\., when driving or cooking etc\., when the device is far away, or any use case involving Siri or Alexa type of devices\. 
- With ChatGPT, it became apparent that native human interface to communicate with each other when we are not next to each other and are not talking is chat, e\.g\., SMS, WhatsApp, Twitter, Slack are all chat interfaces where there is a text box and a stream of messages\. 
	- So what if in some ways the chat interface is the thin client dream, where it's just one UI to every single type of application and you have so all of these modalities \(i\.e\., voice, on the fly generated UI and text\), but it's in a very familiar interface where you interact with any of those applications\. 
- So far there are no clear examples of applications with novel hybrid interfaces like voice and text\. With the likes of Cursor and advanced voice in ChatGPT, at the moment users are essentially interacting with applications based on a mode\. 
	- But there will be a lot of interesting UI explorations over the next few years that treat the modality more fluidly\. When interacting with people, it's very much a mixed modality kind of interaction\. And this just has not been seen in applications yet\. Over time, there will be a blend, especially as computer vision comes more into the mix\.

## Key Claims

1. LiveKit SDK sits on the device and is responsible for accessing the camera and microphone, capturing speech and transmitting it over LiveKit's network.
2. LiveKit operates a global edge network of servers that form a mesh network fabric connecting client devices to backend agents.
3. Traditional voice mode used a speech-to-text step to convert audio to text, which was then sent into an LLM, with output tokens converted back to speech.
4. Advanced voice mode (GPT-4o) processes audio natively, with inference performed directly on audio embeddings rather than converting to text first.
5. LiveKit's agent framework functions analogously to an application server in web development, receiving audio from client devices over the network.
6. LiveKit conceptualizes itself as a nervous system for AI, transporting sensory inputs (audio, video) to the AI 'brain' and returning outputs.
7. If AI systems are effectively equivalent to human brains, the natural interface will be voice, camera, and microphone rather than keyboard and mouse.
8. Voice AI latency was approximately 4 seconds in early 2023 when ChatGPT first launched.
9. Voice AI latency with GPT-4o and LiveKit is now approximately 320 milliseconds on average, a roughly 10-fold decrease from early 2023.
10. Human-level conversational turn-taking speed is approximately 300 milliseconds.

## Capabilities

- Real-time speech-to-speech conversation with AI via multimodal models that process audio directly without text intermediate step
- Voice-based AI tutoring system that can teach on any topic and answer arbitrary questions without judgment
- GUI automation agents that can click buttons, fill forms, navigate web pages, and request human intervention via video streaming when stuck
- Automated telephone-based AI systems for high-volume tasks like insurance eligibility verification without human intermediary
- End-to-end autonomous vehicle driving in complex urban environments with construction, multiple lanes, and unstructured navigation
- Voice control interface for vehicle operations (doors, horn, and other functions via API)
- On-device speech processing models that can run locally with reasonable capability

## Limitations

- AI models still hallucinate and make mistakes, unable to achieve 100% accuracy in customer-facing tasks
- Customer support automation cannot eliminate human staff entirely; requires human standby for corrections and edge cases
- Slower-than-expected AI adoption in workflows despite being fastest-growing consumer app; many aware of ChatGPT but don't actively use it
- Legacy system integration is the primary blocker—not latency. AI needs to update diverse, bespoke backend systems (Salesforce, custom software) to fully automate workflows
- Spiking neural networks are theoretically promising for audio/video but significantly harder to train than Transformers; backpropagation not directly applicable
- Very low inference latency (under 100ms) causes models to interrupt users by responding before they finish speaking
- Voice-only interfaces cannot effectively present menu options; reading entire menu to user creates poor UX; hybrid modalities required
- Local on-device models cannot answer specialized queries requiring latest information (e.g., proprietary router specifications); requires cloud fallback
- Privacy concerns and regulatory requirements create scenarios where data cannot be sent to cloud, limiting use of larger, more capable models
- Voice interfaces fail in noisy/crowded environments; users must fall back to text input as alternative modality
- Companies slow to adopt AI in customer support despite technical capability due to NPS/satisfaction risk with proven incumbent systems

## Bottlenecks

- Enterprise system integration complexity—AI agents must update diverse, bespoke backend systems (Salesforce, custom software) to fully automate customer workflows; this is the actual blocker, not latency
- Training methodology gap for alternative architectures like spiking neural networks; cannot use standard backpropagation, much harder to optimize than Transformers
- Risk aversion and organizational inertia in customer support; companies reluctant to replace working at-scale systems with AI due to NPS/customer satisfaction risk
- Residual hallucination and error rates in AI models require human-in-the-loop supervision in customer-facing applications; cannot achieve 100% automation

## Breakthroughs

- GPT-4o multimodal models with joint text-speech embeddings enabling direct audio processing without text intermediate representation; 10x latency improvement to human-level conversational speed
- Anthropic computer use API enabling vision-capable agents to control GUIs, click buttons, fill forms, with human intervention capability when uncertain
- Tesla full self-driving achieving reliable end-to-end autonomous navigation in complex urban environments with construction, multiple lanes, and unstructured road conditions
