1. **The structural shift it represents** — "service as software" rather than copilot augmentation, where agents replace work rather than assist it. This frames why Agentforce matters beyond being another Salesforce product.

2. **Business model implications** — contrasting the per-seat copilot model (Microsoft's playbook) against the per-outcome agent model (Zendesk, Intercom, Agentforce), with concrete pricing examples from the source material.

3. **Competitive positioning and its limits** — Salesforce's distribution advantage versus the depth disadvantage of a horizontal platform competing against vertical specialists, plus the structural problem that low model-switching costs undermine AI-layer lock-in.

The limitations section surfaces the genuinely open questions: horizontal vs. vertical tension, pricing cannibalization risk, model dependency, and regulatory exposure — areas where the evidence doesn't yet resolve cleanly.
