# Generative Reward Models

GenRM replaces the Bradley-Terry scalar reward model with a fully generative LLM-based preference model trained on self-bootstrapped reasoning traces, achieving parity with classical reward models in-distribution while outperforming them by 10–45% on out-of-distribution tasks — without requiring ground-truth rationales, a stronger teacher model, or architectural overhead.

**Authors:** Dakota Mahan, Duy Van Phung, Rafael Rafailov, Chase Blagden, Nathan Lile, Louis Castricato, Jan-Philipp Fränken, Chelsea Finn, Alon Albalak
**Published:** 2024-10-02
**Type:** paper

---

## Motivation

The standard [[themes/reward_modeling|reward modeling]] pipeline has two competing failure modes. Explicitly parameterized [[themes/alignment_methods|Bradley-Terry reward models]] are strong in-distribution but generalize poorly to novel prompt distributions — a consequence of their fixed scalar parameterization, which structurally cannot represent pluralistic or conflicting preferences. RLAIF approaches using zero-shot LLM-as-a-judge offer better robustness but consistently underperform trained reward models on in-distribution tasks by 9–36%, and their synthetic preference labels don't reliably align with actual human judgments.

GenRM is motivated by the observation that these failure modes are complementary: generative reasoning provides the OOD flexibility that Bradley-Terry lacks, while iterative fine-tuning recovers the in-distribution precision that zero-shot LLM judging lacks.

---

## Approach

GenRM models the full preference distribution p(y_w ≻ y_l | x) directly through the LLM's next-token probabilities over answer indicator tokens, making it strictly more general than Bradley-Terry and compatible with both PPO and iterative *PO optimization. The paper introduces three variants:

1. **GenRM** — LLM fine-tuned as a classifier with no chain-of-thought; outputs preference probabilities via indicator token logits
2. **CoT-GenRM with Rationalization** — trained on reasoning chains from a post-rationalization model via SFT
3. **CoT-GenRM-STaR** — self-bootstraps reasoning chains using STaR, with two sub-variants:
   - **STaR-SFT**: filters for correct-judgment traces and applies SFT
   - **STaR-DPO**: uses correct vs. incorrect reasoning traces as preference pairs under DPO loss over (rationale, answer) sequences, learning from all sampled data rather than discarding wrong-judgment traces

The STaR-DPO variant is the key contribution. Unlike concurrent work (Ankner et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024), it requires no separate strong model for rationale supply, no reference solutions, and no hybrid Bradley-Terry architecture — rationales are fully self-bootstrapped from the same base model.

## Results

### In-Distribution (UltraFeedback)

| Model | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| Base LLM (no CoT) | 52.25% |
| Base LLM + CoT prompting | 67.75% |
| STaR-SFT | 67.4% |
| Bradley-Terry RM | ~73–74% |
| **STaR-DPO** | **~73–74%** |

STaR-DPO matches Bradley-Terry in-distribution. STaR-SFT fails to improve over the base LLM — supervised filtering on correct traces without contrastive signal provides no benefit.

### Out-of-Distribution (RewardBench)

| Model | Overall | Safety | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base LLM-as-judge | 77.8% | — | 76.6% |
| Bradley-Terry RM | ~74–79% | 81.8% | **below random** |
| STaR-Rationalizer | < base LLM | — | — |
| **STaR-DPO** | **81.9%** | **91.0%** | **87.2%** |

The collapse of Bradley-Terry on OOD reasoning is stark: it scores below random chance on RewardBench Reasoning when trained on UltraInteract — catastrophic distribution shift failure. STaR-DPO's 91.0% on Safety indicates that explicit reasoning is especially valuable for safety-critical preference judgments.

### Inference-Time Scaling

Majority voting over 32 sampled traces improves accuracy by 1.6–4.9% across benchmarks, creating an inference-time compute scaling axis for reward models. This mirrors scaling behavior seen in generative reasoning tasks.

## Key Findings on Failure Modes

**STaR-Rationalizer fails OOD despite matching in-distribution.** Training on reasoning traces generated by a post-rationalization model (even a stronger one) fails to generalize, underperforming even the untuned base LLM on held-out benchmarks. The cause is off-policy distribution mismatch: the training rationales are not aligned with the model's own generative distribution. This bottleneck — [[themes/synthetic_data_generation|off-policy trace mismatch]] blocking knowledge distillation for iterative reward training — resolves within months if on-policy training is used.

**Stronger teacher models don't reliably help.** Bootstrapping from LLaMA-3.1-70B or GPT-4 does not consistently outperform self-bootstrapping from the same 8B model. GPT-4-sourced rationales show worse generalization unless supplemented by additional on-policy training. On-policy alignment of critic reasoning is more important than raw rationale quality.

**STaR-SFT has no contrastive signal.** Filtering for only correct traces discards information about what makes reasoning fail. STaR-DPO's insight is to use wrong-judgment traces as the losing side of preference pairs — learning from the full sample distribution rather than throwing half of it away.

## Capabilities

- **Generative preference modeling** that achieves in-distribution parity with Bradley-Terry while outperforming it by 10–45% OOD (maturity: demo)
- **STaR-based self-bootstrapping** enabling reward models to generate their own training rationales without supervision from a stronger model (maturity: demo)
- **Inference-time compute scaling** for reward models via majority voting over sampled reasoning traces (maturity: demo)
- **Safety evaluation transfer**: 91.0% accuracy on safety preferences suggests reasoning-based reward models generalize safety judgments across distributions (maturity: demo)

## Limitations & Open Questions

**Structural limitations of Bradley-Terry (resolved by GenRM):**
- Single scalar point estimate per (prompt, response) pair cannot represent pluralistic or conflicting preferences — a fundamental architectural constraint, not a training failure
- Catastrophic OOD generalization failure, especially on reasoning tasks where it scores below random

**Remaining limitations of GenRM / STaR-DPO:**
- **Inference cost**: 32× overhead from majority voting to realize inference-time scaling gains, versus a single BT forward pass
- **Self-improvement ceiling**: STaR bootstrapping cannot generate training data from examples the model initially fails to solve — hard ceiling on self-improvement from purely self-generated data
- **Adversarial robustness untested**: reward hacking behavior and adversarial probe resistance explicitly deferred as future work, critical for deployment
- **Data selection understudied**: which examples to include across SFT and pairwise DPO rounds in iterative RLAIF has large performance headroom remaining
- **Multimodal extension unexplored**: all results are text-only; VLM alignment and visual reward modeling not attempted

**Systemic issues highlighted:**
- [[themes/alignment_methods|RLAIF]] synthetic preferences systematically misalign with human preference judgments, blocking fully automated RLHF pipelines — a 1–2 year bottleneck for scalable, annotation-free alignment
- [[themes/post_training_methods|Purely offline DPO]] consistently underperforms online RL; state-of-the-art pipelines have already moved to hybrid online strategies
- LLM-as-judge systematically over-weights verbosity, failing to enforce explicit instruction-following constraints
