## Capabilities

- Google and OpenAI both achieved gold-medal performance on the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) using LLMs powered by advanced reasoning and RL techniques — the first time AI systems have ever (maturity: narrow_production)
- RL-trained reasoning models (OpenAI o-series, DeepSeek R1, Gemini 2.0) are in production use for enterprise long-horizon decision-making spanning dozens of systems and thousands of conditional steps. (maturity: narrow_production)
- MoE LLM (355B total, 32B active parameters) unifying reasoning, coding, and agentic capabilities at frontier level, ranking 3rd across 12 benchmarks against models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepM (maturity: narrow_production)
- Small RULER-trained models (Qwen 2.5) outperform much larger frontier models (OpenAI o3) on all 4 evaluated agentic tasks at lower inference cost (maturity: demo)
- Multimodal LLMs can reason about physical common sense (space, time, fundamental physics) from video input using long chain-of-thought, achieving scores competitive with or exceeding OpenAI o1 on stru (maturity: demo)

## Known Limitations

- OpenAI's standalone compute infrastructure creates a structural cost disadvantage against Google, which has custom TPUs, massive distribution, and an established monetization engine (severity: significant, trajectory: worsening)
- Large incumbent platforms (OpenAI, etc.) cannot replicate the deep ERP integration and client-embedded reliability required by vertical AI — their scale advantage does not transfer to last-mile enterp (severity: minor, trajectory: stable)
- Current SOTA multimodal models (GPT-4o, Gemini 2.0 Flash, OpenAI o1) perform at or near random chance on arrow-of-time and object permanence tasks, despite strong performance on standard benchmarks li (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
