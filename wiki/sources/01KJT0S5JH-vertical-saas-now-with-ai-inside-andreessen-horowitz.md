## Briefing

**AI represents a third, and most consequential, wave of value creation for vertical SaaS companies — not by adding more software features, but by converting human labor directly into software revenue. Where fintech doubled or tripled VSaaS take rates by embedding financial services, AI could expand them by 2-10x further by automating the operational workforce that customers still need to run their businesses. The macro opportunity is enormous: US labor spend ($10.5T) dwarfs software spend ($313B) by 33x, implying VSaaS has barely begun to capture its addressable value.**

### Key Takeaways
1. **The third VSaaS wave converts labor to software** — AI enables vertical SaaS platforms to automate tasks in sales, marketing, customer service, operations, and finance that were previously too complex for software to handle.
2. **2-10x take rate expansion is the core revenue thesis** — just as fintech added 2-5x revenue per customer in wave two, AI is projected to add another 2-10x multiplier on top of existing VSaaS monetization.
3. **Software spend is only 3% of labor spend** — US software spend was $313B vs. $10.5T in labor in 2022, meaning even modest labor displacement represents massive VSaaS expansion headroom.
4. **Toast's fintech success is the precedent** — Toast reached $1.5B ARR with 80%+ of revenue from fintech, proving that embedding non-software value layers can structurally reshape VSaaS economics.
5. **Winner-take-most dynamics amplify AI advantages** — the VSaaS player that embeds AI most effectively in a given vertical is likely to extend its dominant position, compounding the stakes of execution.
6. **Labor displacement is function-specific, not total** — roles without human connection value (admin, customer service, finance, marketing) are displacement candidates; roles where human connection is the product (fitness instructors) are not.
7. **Mindbody's unit economics illustrate the thesis concretely** — saving one $60K/year admin worker per studio, at 10% value capture, doubles Mindbody's ~$6K ACV; full capture of labor savings across functions implies 10x ACV.
8. **VSaaS AI opens markets previously unviable** — beyond per-customer expansion, AI reduces the cost structure of serving small businesses, potentially unlocking verticals previously too small or too expensive to acquire.
9. **The Mindbody case study generalizes broadly** — any VSaaS serving SMBs in labor-intensive verticals (fitness, beauty, home services, restaurants) faces the same opportunity structure.
10. **This is Part 1 of 3** — the piece explicitly defers the new-market-opening dimension of AI to subsequent installments, meaning the revenue-per-customer argument here is only half the thesis.

### The Three-Wave Framework for VSaaS Evolution

- Vertical SaaS has scaled through three distinct waves, each layering new value on top of the prior infrastructure.
  - **Wave 1 — Cloud:** Brought industry workflows online; core examples are Shopify (e-commerce) and ServiceTitan (service workers), which digitized previously offline operations.
  - **Wave 2 — Cloud + Fintech:** Embedded financial services (payments, lending, payroll, insurance) inside the software, allowing VSaaS to capture a share of customer financial flows, not just subscription fees.
    - Toast is the flagship example: $1.5B ARR, with **over 80% of revenue from financial technology solutions** rather than software subscriptions.
    - The fintech layer enabled 2-5x revenue per customer expansion and opened markets previously uneconomical to serve.
  - **Wave 3 — Cloud + Fintech + AI:** Described as "the most impactful force in the category to date," this wave expands VSaaS surface area by turning labor into software.
    - The defining mechanism: AI enables VSaaS platforms to take on operational tasks that were previously too complex for software and required human workers.

### The Labor-to-Software Conversion Thesis

- The central claim is that VSaaS companies can use AI to absorb the labor spend of their customers — capturing that value as higher-priced software rather than leaving it with external labor markets.
  - Customers of VSaaS currently still hire people to handle marketing, sales, customer service, operations, and finance — functions the software doesn't fully automate.
  - AI changes the capability boundary of software, making rote tasks in these functions automatable for the first time.
- **The macro framing is striking:** total US software spend ($313B) was just 3% of total US labor spend ($10.5T) in 2022.
  - This ratio implies that even a small shift of labor spend into software spend represents many multiples of the current software market.
  - VSaaS companies with deep customer workflows and high switching costs are structurally best positioned to capture this shift in their verticals.
- The projected financial impact: **2-10x increase in take rate** on top of the existing VSaaS revenue base.

### The Mindbody Case Study: Quantifying the Opportunity

- Mindbody serves fitness and beauty studios and illustrates each wave concretely.
  - **Wave 1:** Booking and scheduling appointments online.
  - **Wave 2:** Payroll for staff, customer payment processing, insurance for studios.
  - **Wave 3 (AI):** Automating operational roles in marketing, sales, customer service, and finance that studios still employ humans to perform.
- **Unit economics worked out in the footnotes:**
  - Average ACV: ~$6K ($500/month, split between payments and subscriptions).
  - Fitness/recreational sports centers employ 100,000+ office and admin workers in receptions, customer service, and information tasks.
  - Saving one $60K/year admin worker per studio and capturing **10% of that value** → additional $6K ACV → **doubles Mindbody's per-customer revenue**.
  - Capturing **100% of savings** from one work

## Key Claims

1. Adding fintech to SaaS businesses can increase revenue per customer by 2-5x and open new SaaS markets previously inaccessible due to market size or customer acquisition cost.
2. Vertical software markets tend to have winner-take-most dynamics, where the best-serving VSaaS company becomes the dominant vertical solution.
3. Toast is at $1.5 billion ARR and makes over 80% of its revenue from financial technology solutions.
4. The third wave of VSaaS (cloud + fintech + AI) is the most impactful force in the category to date.
5. AI should increase the take rate of VSaaS companies by an additional 2-10x.
6. Roles where human connection isn't a key benefit are candidates to be augmented or fully replaced by AI.
7. Total US software spend ($313bn) was just 3% the size of total labor spend ($10.5 trillion) in 2022.
8. Fitness and recreational sports centers employ more than 100,000 office and administrative workers across receptions, customer service, and information tasks.
9. Mindbody's average ACV is approximately $6k ($500 per month), split between payments and subscriptions.
10. By saving one worker at $60k per year per fitness business and capturing 10% of that value, Mindbody could double its ACV by an additional $6k.

## Capabilities

- AI can automate rote operational functions within vertical SaaS contexts — marketing, sales, customer service, operations, and finance — enabling VSaaS platforms to replace or augment previously human-only tasks and capture the resulting labor cost savings as additional ARR
- Vertical SaaS platforms embedding AI automation can increase revenue per customer by 2–10x by converting customer labor spend into software spend, unlocking a market headroom that dwarfs traditional SaaS TAM

## Limitations

- AI cannot replace human workers in roles where personal relationship quality and emotional connection are core to the product experience
- The degree to which VSaaS platforms can monetise AI labor displacement through pricing is highly uncertain — modelled capture rates span an order of magnitude (10% to 100%) with no empirical basis cited
- Broad AI labor replacement within VSaaS is explicitly nascent — framed as a wave just beginning, not a demonstrated at-scale production reality
- Full elimination of labor across VSaaS business operations is explicitly an imagined future scenario, not a current or near-term capability
- No accuracy, error rate, reliability, or failure mode data is cited for AI automating customer-facing or financial operations within VSaaS — the investment thesis is entirely silent on production reliability
- No regulatory, compliance, or legal analysis accompanies the AI labor replacement thesis — implications for regulated adjacent verticals (financial services within VSaaS, healthcare-adjacent wellness) are entirely absent
- No named production examples of VSaaS companies successfully capturing AI-driven labor displacement as additional ARR are cited — the entire thesis rests on a single hypothetical Mindbody model

## Bottlenecks

- VSaaS platforms must cross an unproven customer trust threshold before buyers will accept AI replacing human workers in operations — willingness to pay for AI labor automation is not yet established, blocking monetisation of the displacement opportunity
- VSaaS pricing models are designed for software unit economics (avg $6k ACV) while the labor displacement opportunity is orders of magnitude larger ($10.5tn US labor vs $313bn software) — bridging this gap requires new pricing structures and value capture mechanisms that do not yet exist
