## Overview

A conditional generative world model by Google DeepMind (Ball et al., 2025) capable of generating real-time interactive photorealistic 3D environments conditioned on text descriptions or initial frames, enabling evaluation of embodied agents in infinitely novel worlds.

## Key Findings

1. SIMA 1 was limited to short and direct instructions, could not respond in language or reason about its actions, and often displayed brittleness in generalizing to new situations or instructions. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
2. Maintaining a mixture of gameplay and non-gameplay Gemini pretraining data during finetuning is crucial to preserve the base model's original capabilities such as vision understanding, dialogue, and r (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
3. SIMA 2 is built upon a Gemini Flash-Lite foundation model and trained using supervised finetuning on a mixture of gameplay and Gemini pretraining (non-gameplay) data. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
4. SIMA 2 substantially closes the gap with human performance across a diverse portfolio of games. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
5. SIMA 1 was incapable of outputting text such as internal reasoning or dialogue, and incapable of receiving multi-modal instruction prompts. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
6. SIMA 1's language encoding was trained from scratch, constraining instruction-following capabilities to the vocabulary of annotated gameplay on which it was trained. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
7. SIMA 2 demonstrates robust generalization to previously unseen environments including photorealistic worlds generated on-the-fly by Genie 3. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
8. The self-improvement process uses three foundation models (task setter, agent, reward model) together with a general world model to autonomously acquire new skills in new environments. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
9. The action space encompasses 96 standard keyboard keys, mouse clicks, and discretized mouse movements representing relative (x, y) position changes, generated as structured text output rather than dis (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
10. Bridge data — synthetically generated by Gemini Pro to annotate human gameplay with causally consistent internal reasoning and dialogue — is necessary to train agents that can simultaneously act, reas (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
11. RL training for SIMA 2 is limited to training environments and explicitly excludes held-out environments such as ASKA and MineDojo. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
12. SIMA 2 uses internal reasoning to modify its own behavior, enabling it to handle indirect, nuanced, or novel instructions not present in training data. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
13. SIMA 2 is capable of open-ended self-improvement by leveraging Gemini to generate tasks and provide rewards, enabling autonomous skill learning from scratch in new environments. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")
14. SIMA 2's agent-environment interface uses only visual input (RGB video at 720p) and keyboard-and-mouse actions, with no privileged information from the environment. (from "SIMA 2: A Generalist Embodied Agent for Virtual Worlds")

## Capabilities

- Google Genie 3, the most capable interactive world model as of publication date, maintains coherent interactive world generation for a few minutes (maturity: demo)

## Known Limitations

- Significant quality gap between Genie 3 and photorealism threshold for some use cases; while improved from Genie 2, still not consistently photorealistic across all scenarios (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
- World model serving economics are unsustainable: Genie 3 ~$100/hr per user, Odyssey requires a full H200 per user — multiple orders of magnitude above LLM serving costs (severity: blocking, trajectory: improving)
