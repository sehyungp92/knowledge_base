# Coatue's Laffont Brothers: AI, Public & VC Markets, Macro, US Debt, Crypto, IPOs, & More | BG2

Coatue's Philippe and Thomas Laffont offer a data-driven investor perspective on the AI supercycle, drawing on proprietary datasets (100 million daily credit card receipts, email receipt analysis) to quantify what was previously anecdotal: that ChatGPT is materially displacing Google Search. The conversation ranges from the structural economics of AI infrastructure to stablecoin regulation, Bitcoin's maturation as an asset class, and the organizational challenge of capturing AI productivity gains.

**Authors:** Philippe Laffont, Thomas Laffont
**Published:** 2025-06-20
**Type:** Video

---

## The AI Supercycle: Structural Context

AI is framed not as an isolated phenomenon but as the latest link in a compounding chain of technology waves — mainframes → networked PCs → internet → SaaS → AI — each built on the infrastructure of the last. This compounding effect accelerates transformative potential with each successive wave.

Technology has grown from 5% to 15% of global GDP over the past few decades. The projection that AI could reach 75% of total US market cap signals not just the rise of AI companies, but AI's capacity to redefine value across all industries.

A notable divergence is already visible: the Magnificent 7 was essentially flat year-over-year while AI pure-plays and AI infrastructure companies — along with private companies like [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] and [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]] — saw significant value accretion. This suggests value creation is dispersing away from legacy tech giants toward purpose-built AI players.

> "Sometimes you make some venture bets and they don't work and then you're like, I just invested in the wrong company in the right trend."

The risk of mistaking a bad investment for a bad trend — or vice versa — is a recurring theme.

## Proving AI's Impact on Google: The Data Science Unlock

One of the most analytically substantive contributions of this source is a quantified treatment of ChatGPT's displacement of Google Search — something Google publicly denied and analysts struggled to prove.

**Method:** Coatue joined two independent datasets — 100 million daily credit card receipts (consumer spending behavior) and email receipt data (subscription tracking) — to isolate the behavior of users who paid for ChatGPT versus those who did not.

**Findings:**
- Users who did *not* subscribe to ChatGPT: Google page view growth of ~4% per year
- Users who subscribed to ChatGPT ($20/month): Google page views declined 8–11% year-over-year, measured over nearly two years

> "Data is useless unless you can join datasets that don't speak to each other. That is the unlock."

This methodological insight — that the value of data lies in its joinability, not its volume — is framed as a general principle in data science.

**Limitation:** Establishing numerical proof of AI's impact on incumbent platforms is inherently difficult. The key unlock required proprietary, high-resolution data that most analysts lack. This implies that public-market understanding of AI displacement lags reality. See [[themes/ai_market_dynamics|AI Market Dynamics]].

**Open question:** Google retains enormous strategic assets — Android, Gmail, YouTube, Waymo — beyond search. The displacement of search does not automatically determine Google's overall trajectory.

## Infrastructure: GPU Allocation as the New Market Signal

A key structural observation concerns the misalignment between cloud market share and Nvidia GPU allocation:

- Amazon: ~44% of cloud revenue market share, but only ~20% of Nvidia GPU allocation
- Oracle: jumped from ~5% to ~19% GPU allocation
- CoreWeave: emerged from near-zero to significant GPU share despite being a new entrant

The thesis: Nvidia GPU allocation is a leading indicator of future AI cloud revenue, not current cloud market share. Companies positioning now — even new entrants — may displace established cloud leaders in the AI infrastructure layer.

[[entities/coreweave|CoreWeave]] is highlighted as one of very few public-market pure-plays on AI infrastructure — a structural advantage over MAG-7 companies whose legacy businesses face disruption risk even as they invest in AI.

**Bottleneck:** A hardware battle is underway between Nvidia GPUs, Google TPUs, and custom chips. The incompatibility of these strategies across providers creates fragmentation risk and prevents uniform scaling of AI services. Resolution horizon: 1–2 years. See [[themes/ai_market_dynamics|AI Market Dynamics]].

## AI Productivity: The Headcount Decoupling

A pattern is emerging across AI-forward companies: revenue growing at 20%+ compounded while headcount and OpEx grow at ~2%. This decoupling of revenue from headcount has no historical precedent in the technology industry.

The mechanism is AI-augmented development and automation — developers produce significantly more output per person, allowing companies to maintain or grow revenue while flat-lining hiring. Salesforce is cited as an example, where leadership is explicitly targeting AI talent and restructuring organizational incentives around AI capability.

**Limitation — uneven capture:** AI productivity gains are not uniformly achievable. Successful implementation appears concentrated in companies with specific characteristics: leadership commitment, organizational willingness to restructure, and tolerance for short-term disruption. Legacy organizational structures and incentive systems create inertia that blocks widespread realization of productivity gains. Bottleneck resolution horizon: 3–5 years.

**Limitation — urgency without deliberation:** The rapid pace of AI capability improvement pressures companies into expensive strategic decisions without normal deliberation time.

> "I don't have two years to wait in European regulatory purgatory."

The AI talent scarcity compounds this: extreme valuation pressure on AI talent creates a second bottleneck on enterprise deployment speed. See [[themes/startup_and_investment|Startup and Investment]].

## ChatGPT's Scale and Competitive Resilience

ChatGPT is scaling toward approximately 1 billion users — a consumer adoption curve with no prior parallel in software. Critically, this growth has not flattened despite the entry of well-resourced competitors: Meta (Llama), Google (Gemini), xAI (Grok).

**Limitation:** Alternative LLMs from major incumbents have not matched ChatGPT's capability or adoption despite significant engineering investment. This is framed not as a permanent advantage but as evidence that first-mover scale and product iteration matter more than raw resource availability. See [[themes/ai_market_dynamics|AI Market Dynamics]].

**Breakthrough:** The combination of consumer scale and demonstrated search displacement constitutes a paradigm-shifting development — the first credible evidence that AI assistants can structurally compete with and displace the dominant information access mechanism of the prior technology era.

## Stablecoins and Crypto: Regulatory Maturation

The GENIUS Act (passed by the Senate) establishes a federal regulatory framework for payment stablecoins, representing a major shift from government antagonism to government support. Regulatory risk was previously a primary constraint on institutional crypto adoption; that constraint is weakening.

**Key distinctions:**
- **Stablecoins** — high utility for payments and business workflows; practical adoption vector; distinct from speculative assets
- **Bitcoin** — ~$2 trillion market cap against ~$450–500 trillion global net worth (~$120T equities, ~$100T real estate, ~$15–20T gold); long-term growth potential framed as comparable to top companies despite being a non-company asset
- **Altcoins/meme coins** — not substantively discussed; implicitly lower-utility

**Current regulatory quirk:** Stablecoins can offer rewards but cannot pay interest, positioning them as exchange media rather than investment instruments. This is flagged as potentially temporary.

**Speculative trajectory:** If stablecoins can pay interest, a path opens where governments issue tokenized treasuries — allowing direct global investment in US bonds, bypassing traditional dealers. This would represent a structural shift in sovereign debt financing.

**Bitcoin volatility:** Evidence of decreasing volatility in recent periods may support broader institutional adoption, though this claim is presented anecdotally.

## Investor Epistemology: Public vs. Private Markets

A structural observation about risk tolerance across investor types:

> "One of my biggest lessons looking at private market investors versus public market investors is that public market institutional investors have very low appetite for assets with perceived 70–80% downside that are marked to market."

This explains why Bitcoin and high-volatility assets struggle to gain institutional allocation despite compelling long-term theses — the mark-to-market mechanism imposes psychological costs that private market investors do not face. The maturation of Bitcoin's volatility profile is therefore a precondition for meaningful institutional allocation, not just regulatory clarity.

## Open Questions

- Will Google's non-search assets (YouTube, Android, Waymo) be sufficient to offset structural search displacement?
- At what point does stablecoin regulation evolve to permit interest-bearing instruments, and what does that do to traditional banking and sovereign debt markets?
- Can AI productivity gains diffuse beyond early-adopter organizations, or do structural/cultural barriers create persistent inequality in AI capture?
- Does Nvidia GPU allocation remain a reliable leading indicator of cloud revenue, or does the multi-chip hardware battle fragment the signal?
- Will Bitcoin's volatility continue to decline at a rate sufficient to unlock meaningful institutional allocation in the near term?
