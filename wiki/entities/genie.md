## Overview

A general world model baseline using masked autoregressive approach, compared against RoboScape on video generation quality metrics.

## Key Findings

1. Dream Machine is a foundational video generative model supporting both text-to-video and image-to-video generation. (from "Luma's Dream Machine and Reasoning in Video Models")
2. Dream Machine is a foundational video generative model supporting both text-to-video and image-to-video generation. (from "Luma's Dream Machine and Reasoning in Video Models")
3. LAPA uses a VQ-VAE-based objective to learn discrete latent actions between image frames in a fully unsupervised manner. (from "Latent Action Pretraining from Videos")
4. RoboScape is a physics-informed world model that jointly learns RGB video generation and physics knowledge through two auxiliary tasks: temporal depth prediction and adaptive keypoint dynamics learnin (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
5. LAPA pretrains a latent VLA model to predict discrete latent actions from observations and task descriptions, then finetunes on small-scale robot data to map latent to robot actions. (from "Latent Action Pretraining from Videos")
6. RoboScape achieves a Pearson correlation of 0.953 and R² of 0.908 with ground-truth simulator outcomes in policy evaluation, while IRASim and iVideoGPT achieve negative correlations (-0.134 and -0.195 (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
7. LAPA is the first unsupervised method for pretraining Vision-Language-Action models without ground-truth robot action labels. (from "Latent Action Pretraining from Videos")
8. RoboScape is trained on 50,000 video clips from AgiBotWorld-Beta covering 147 tasks and 72 skills, preprocessed into approximately 6.5 million 16-frame clips sampled at 2Hz. (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
9. LAPA outperforms OPENVLA (current state-of-the-art VLA) by +6.22% while achieving over 30x greater pretraining efficiency. (from "Latent Action Pretraining from Videos")
10. Diffusion Policy trained on 200 synthetic trajectories generated by RoboScape achieves 91% success rate on Robomimic Lift, approaching the 92% achieved with 200 real trajectories. (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
11. pi0 trained with 800 synthetic trajectories from RoboScape achieves 79.1% average success on LIBERO tasks, compared to 65.2% with 200 real trajectories only. (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
12. RoboScape training completes in approximately 24 hours on a cluster of 32 NVIDIA A800-SXM4-80GB GPUs. (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
13. Existing VLA models require action labels collected by human teleoperators during pretraining, which significantly limits possible data sources and scale. (from "Latent Action Pretraining from Videos")
14. RoboScape uses a dual-branch co-autoregressive Transformer (DCT) for joint prediction of RGB and depth, where depth features are injected into the RGB branch via cross-branch interaction pathways to e (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")
15. RoboScape outperforms all baselines across six evaluation metrics (LPIPS, PSNR, AbsRel, δ1, δ2, ΔPSNR), achieving LPIPS of 0.1259, PSNR of 21.8533, and ΔPSNR of 3.3435. (from "RoboScape: Physics-informed Embodied World Model")

## Capabilities

- Google Genie 3, the most capable interactive world model as of publication date, maintains coherent interactive world generation for a few minutes (maturity: demo)

## Known Limitations

- Significant quality gap between Genie 3 and photorealism threshold for some use cases; while improved from Genie 2, still not consistently photorealistic across all scenarios (severity: significant, trajectory: improving)
- World model serving economics are unsustainable: Genie 3 ~$100/hr per user, Odyssey requires a full H200 per user — multiple orders of magnitude above LLM serving costs (severity: blocking, trajectory: improving)
