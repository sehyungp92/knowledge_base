# ThinkMorph: Emergent Properties in Multimodal Interleaved Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

### Motivation & Prior Limitations
Textual Chain-of-Thought has advanced verbal reasoning but contributes little to multimodal tasks that require interrogating or manipulating visual content, leaving models unable to handle problems demanding more than textual description.
- Tool-augmented interleaved reasoning (e.g., external cropping tools, specialized sketching models) is indirect and brittle, as the reasoning process depends on fragile external module calls.
- Unified model approaches lack a generalizable recipe: MVoT introduced interleaved action representations for maze-solving but confined textual reasoning to simplistic action labels isomorphic to generated images, showing no generalization beyond training domains.
- Prior work on large-scale interleaved datasets (Zebra-CoT) did not rigorously examine data quality or test out-of-domain generalization, leaving open the central question of when multimodal CoT actually outperforms text-only or visual-only reasoning.
- The key theoretical gap is treating text and images as isomorphic rather than complementary modalities: if both modalities encode the same information in different forms, interleaving adds no reasoning value.

---

### Proposed Approach
ThinkMorph is a unified model fine-tuned on ~24K high-quality interleaved reasoning traces across four tasks with varying visual engagement, built on the principle that text and image tokens must be complementary rather than isomorphic — each modality advancing what the other cannot express alone.
- Unlike tool-augmented designs, ThinkMorph uses a single unified model (Bagel-7B base) that natively generates both text tokens and image tokens within a single autoregressive sequence, with modality transitions controlled by `<image_start>` and `<image_end>` delimiter tokens.
- The four training tasks are carefully chosen to require different degrees of visual engagement: Jigsaw Assembly (re-arranging patches to reveal spatial coherence), Spatial Navigation (overlaying path trajectories on maze grids), Visual Search (drawing bounding boxes to localize objects), and Chart Refocus (highlighting relevant chart regions before value extraction). Each task is designed so that image thoughts provide information text cannot encode, not merely a restatement of the text.
- Training uses dual objectives simultaneously: MSE loss on image tokens and cross-entropy loss on text tokens, optimizing both generation and understanding jointly. Data curation for Visual Search reduced a 144K-sample pool to 6,990 high-quality examples by enforcing that target object bounding boxes occupy 1–30% of image area, filtering ambiguous questions and incorrect answers via MLLM-in-the-loop review.
- The dataset includes derived unimodal baselines — text-only traces generated by GPT-4.1 and visual-only traces extracted from interleaved sequences — enabling controlled ablations across all three reasoning modes on identical tasks.

### Results & Capabilities
ThinkMorph achieves an average gain of 34.74% over the Bagel-7B base model on vision-centric benchmarks, with the most dramatic improvement on Spatial Navigation (+85.84%, from 0.83% to 86.67%) and strong gains on Jigsaw Assembly (+38.75% in-domain).
- Interleaved reasoning outperforms both text-only and visual-only modes by an average of 5.33% across vision-centric tasks; the advantage is largest when visual tokens enable steps text cannot perform (e.g., re-arranged puzzle pieces revealing mismatches, overlaid arrows validating navigation routes).
- On out-of-domain benchmarks ThinkMorph surpasses InternVL3.5-38B on SAT spatial reasoning (52.67% vs. 49.33%), outperforms Qwen2.5-VL-72B on VSP by 34% and on BLINK-J by 10.67%, and matches Gemini 2.5 Flash on MMVP perception (80.33%), despite being a 7B model fine-tuned on only 24K samples.
- **Emergent Property 1 — Unseen Visual Manipulations:** On out-of-domain benchmarks the model generates visual operations never seen during training, including zoom-in, inpainting, multi-box generation, motion forecasting, perspective transformation, and region cropping. These account for up to 10% of all visual operations at inference and are triggered reliably by specific textual phrases (e.g., "examine closely" → zoom-in; "restore" → inpainting), suggesting that Bagel's pretraining supplied latent manipulation capability that interleaved fine-tuning directed toward structured reasoning steps.
- **Emergent Property 2 — Autonomous Mode Switching:** Despite being trained exclusively on interleaved traces, ThinkMorph autonomously switches to text-only reasoning on 5.3% of inference cases on MMVP; these switched instances reach 81.25% accuracy vs. 73.96% for the same samples solved with interleaved reasoning — a 7.29% gain — and use approximately 75% fewer tokens (89 vs. 156), confirming that the model learns to avoid redundant visual computation when initial perceptual encoding suffices.
- **Emergent Property 3 — Better Test-Time Scaling via Diversified Thoughts:** Under Best-of-N sampling, interleaved reasoning maintains consistent gains across all benchmarks — +5.2% on VSP, +1.0% on VStar, +0.7% on MMVP, +8.0% on BLINK-J at N=8 — while visual reasoning degrades on BLINK-J (−2.0%) and text reasoning improves only modestly (+2.67%), showing that multimodal trajectory diversity becomes most valuable under distribution shift.

### Implications
The complementarity principle — that interleaved modalities must each advance what the other cannot express — offers a concrete design criterion for multimodal reasoning data curation, replacing the vague goal of "adding images to chain-of-thought."

## Key Claims

1. Text-only Chain-of-Thought contributes little to multimodal reasoning when problems demand more than textual description, motivating a shift to genuinely cross-modal reasoning.
2. ThinkMorph is a unified model fine-tuned on approximately 24K high-quality interleaved reasoning traces spanning four tasks with varying levels of visual engagement.
3. ThinkMorph achieves an average 34.74% improvement over its base model on vision-centric benchmarks.
4. MVoT's textual component is confined to simplistic action labels isomorphic to its generated images, showing little generalization beyond training domains.
5. ThinkMorph achieves 85.84% improvement on Spatial Navigation over the base model, which nearly fails at 0.83%.
6. ThinkMorph achieves 38.75% in-domain improvement on Jigsaw Assembly over the base model.
7. Interleaved reasoning outperforms the next-best unimodal reasoning mode by 5.33% across vision-centric tasks.
8. Interleaved reasoning surpasses text-only reasoning by 6.33% on out-of-domain MMVP, demonstrating better generalization when visual grounding is essential.
9. Specific textual cues reliably trigger corresponding visual manipulations: 'examine closely' or 'focus on' consistently elicit zoom-in operations, while 'restore' and 'reconstruct' prompt image inpain
10. ThinkMorph autonomously switches from interleaved to text-only reasoning in 5.3% of inference cases despite being trained exclusively on interleaved data.

## Capabilities

- Interleaved text-image chain-of-thought reasoning in a 7B unified model achieves 34.74% average improvement over a base model on vision-centric benchmarks, by generating progressive reasoning steps that manipulate visual content alongside verbal logic rather than treating modalities as isomorphic
- Interleaved fine-tuning on 24K examples causes a 7B unified model to spontaneously generate visual manipulation operations (zoom-in, inpainting, bounding boxes, motion forecasting, perspective shift, region crop) that were never seen during training, triggered by contextually appropriate textual cue
- A model trained exclusively on interleaved reasoning traces learns to autonomously switch to text-only reasoning when visual engagement is redundant, producing a 7.29% accuracy improvement on those instances while consuming approximately 75% fewer tokens
- Interleaved multimodal reasoning enables superior Best-of-N test-time scaling compared to unimodal approaches, with +8.0% accuracy gain at N=8 on BLINK-J (the hardest out-of-domain benchmark) while visual-only reasoning degrades by 2.0% under the same regime
- A 7B unified model fine-tuned on 24K interleaved traces achieves 86.67% on Spatial Navigation (up from a base model near-zero at 0.83%), surpasses Qwen2.5-VL-72B on VSP by 34%, exceeds InternVL3.5-38B on SAT, and matches Gemini 2.5 Flash on MMVP
- Mode diversity in test-time sampling (models flexibly switching between textual and interleaved reasoning per sample) amplifies the benefits of Best-of-N scaling beyond what single-mode sampling achieves

## Limitations

- Text-only chain-of-thought reasoning contributes little to tasks requiring genuine visual engagement — models that rely solely on textual reasoning still fail on spatial navigation, fine-grained perception, and visual manipulation tasks where text cannot express solution-critical cues
- Tool-augmented multimodal reasoning designs that delegate visual operations to external modules are indirect and brittle, creating fragile pipelines that degrade when the visual sub-module fails or produces unexpected outputs
- Defining what constitutes meaningful interleaved visual reasoning is fundamentally hard, making it difficult to set quality criteria or collect data at scale — visual thinking cannot be easily externalized into verifiable intermediate representations
- Joint training of multimodal understanding and generation in unified models is fragile: optimizing generative objectives tends to degrade understanding capabilities and vice versa, making unified model training brittle
- Interleaved reasoning provides no benefit — and may slightly hurt performance — on tasks where textual reasoning alone already captures the relevant information; the overhead of generating image tokens is wasteful in these cases
- Perception-focused benchmarks exhibit non-monotonic (U-shaped) test-time scaling — intermediate sampling counts (N=2, N=4) initially hurt accuracy before larger N recovers, suggesting interleaved reasoning's test-time scaling benefit is not reliable at small N for perceptual tasks
- ThinkMorph's training is confined to four narrow task types (jigsaw assembly, spatial navigation, visual search, chart refocus), raising questions about how well the interleaved reasoning approach generalises to the full diversity of visual tasks beyond these controlled categories
- Emergent visual manipulation capabilities are contingent on the base model having been pretrained on sufficiently diverse interleaved visual-text data — models without this pretraining foundation cannot develop these skills through interleaved fine-tuning alone
- Prior interleaved reasoning approaches (MVoT) that treat text and image as isomorphic representations — where text describes the same content as images rather than providing complementary information — show little generalisation beyond their training distributions
- ThinkMorph still underperforms larger VLMs on pure visual understanding tasks (VStar: 67.02 vs Qwen2.5-VL-72B's 85.86; CV-Bench: 80.82 vs 82.54), suggesting that interleaved reasoning benefits are concentrated in reasoning-intensive rather than perception-intensive evaluation

## Bottlenecks

- Scaling interleaved multimodal reasoning training is bottlenecked by the difficulty of constructing high-quality interleaved traces at scale — visual thinking cannot be easily externalised or verified, and current data synthesis pipelines required heavy filtering (144K → 6,990 usable examples for on
- Joint understanding-generation training in unified multimodal models creates an optimisation conflict where improving generation quality degrades understanding representations and vice versa, blocking development of models that simultaneously excel at both
- Fine-grained spatial and visual manipulation tasks remain a data bottleneck — internet-scale pretraining corpora lack the task-specific interleaved traces (path overlays, bounding box manipulation, image inpainting as reasoning steps) needed to directly learn from gradient signal on these tasks

## Breakthroughs

- Interleaved text-image fine-tuning on only 24K examples causes a 7B unified model to develop emergent visual manipulation capabilities not present in training data, autonomous reasoning mode selection, and superior test-time scaling — matching or exceeding proprietary models and models 5-10x larger
