# Reasoning & Planning

> Reasoning & Planning sits at an uncomfortable inflection point: neural approaches have closed measurable gaps on abstract reasoning benchmarks, yet the field is accumulating evidence that what looks like planning may be sophisticated pattern-matching with a hard ceiling. The clearest signal is the divergence between benchmark progress and generalisation: large reasoning models (LRMs) can execute hundreds of sequential correct moves on well-trained tasks, yet collapse on structurally similar problems underrepresented in training — and providing the explicit solution algorithm does not help. Momentum is building in unexpected directions, particularly the discovery that strong chain-of-thought reasoning can bootstrap richer pretraining data without external supervision, potentially shifting reasoning capability investment upstream into data structure rather than post-training methods.

## Current State

The trajectory of reasoning research runs most sharply through ARC as a proxy for abstract reasoning. By late 2024, test-time training (TTT) had pushed neural LLMs to 73.5% coverage of tasks solvable by program synthesis — up from 42.2% without it — a meaningful compression of the neural-symbolic gap that had long favoured engineered approaches. Yet that same result exposed the ceiling: best human performance sits at 97.8%, leaving a ~36-point gulf that TTT does not close. Program synthesis, meanwhile, remains bottlenecked not by lack of cleverness but by combinatorial enumeration — the transformation space is near-infinite even under strong priors, and no performant search strategy exists. That bottleneck has not moved; its 3-5 year horizon reflects genuine algorithmic intractability, not engineering lag.

The more unsettling development came in mid-2025, when controlled puzzle environments revealed that LRMs achieve strong performance on Tower of Hanoi (up to 100 sequential correct moves at N=10) but collapse on structurally similar tasks like River Crossing that are underrepresented in training data. Critically, providing the explicit solution algorithm does not help. This is the sharpest signal in the current data: LRM "planning" appears to be retrieval of trained patterns rather than generalizable algorithmic execution. The architectural limitation is classified as blocking, and its trajectory remains unclear because there is no obvious intervention that addresses it.

Momentum is building in one unexpected direction: the cross-theme link to synthetic data suggests that strong CoT reasoning can bootstrap richer training examples without an external supervisor, potentially shifting reasoning capability investment upstream into pretraining data structure rather than post-training methods. Strategic augmentation at inference time has also emerged as a high-leverage lever — AIRV achieved a 260% accuracy boost via augmentation and ensemble voting, reframing augmentation-and-vote as a first-class inference strategy. And the discovery that software engineering capability generalises into broader multi-step problem-solving implies that SE agent benchmarks may be systematically underestimating general reasoning capability.

The near-term signal to watch: whether any intervention breaks the "algorithm provision doesn't help" finding. If LRMs remain impervious to explicit algorithmic guidance, the path to reliable agentic pipelines requiring procedural execution narrows considerably, and the verification bottleneck in judgment-dependent domains becomes the dominant constraint on AI autonomy expansion.

## Capabilities

- **Neural LLMs with test-time training reach 73.5% of program synthesis coverage on ARC** (up from 42.2% without TTT), demonstrating meaningful compression of the neural-symbolic gap. *(maturity: research_only)*
- **LLMs trained with multi-task and code pre-training** (e.g., LongT5 encoder-decoder) can acquire contextual, associative reasoning capabilities. *(maturity: research_only)*
- **LRMs can execute up to ~100 sequential correct moves** in well-represented planning tasks (Tower of Hanoi N=10), demonstrating reliable sequential execution within their training distribution. *(maturity: demo)*

## Limitations

- **Algorithm provision does not improve LRM performance** — providing the explicit solution algorithm does not help models succeed on tasks where they currently fail; they still fail at the same complexity threshold. *(severity: blocking, trajectory: unclear, type: explicit)*
- **GPQA graduate-level science QA at 79.1%** — a 5-9 point gap behind Grok 4 (87.7%) and Gemini 2.5 Pro (84.4%), indicating persistent ceiling effects on expert-level reasoning. *(severity: significant, trajectory: improving, type: implicit_performance_cliff)*
- **HLE (Humanity's Last Exam) score of 14.4%** reveals a significant performance cliff on genuinely hard, novel cross-domain problems that are outside training distribution. *(severity: significant, trajectory: improving, type: implicit_performance_cliff)*
- **~36-point gap on ARC** — best human performance is 97.8% vs. 61.9% achieved by SOTA systems, indicating a fundamental ceiling in abstract generalisation. *(severity: significant, trajectory: improving, type: implicit_performance_cliff)*
- **Object-centric neural approaches fail to generalise** when the task-relevant 'object' is ambiguous or highly context-dependent. *(severity: significant, trajectory: stable, type: explicit)*
- **Program synthesis requires extensive manual engineering** — humans must explicitly encode pair-association heuristics; the approach does not self-improve. *(severity: significant, trajectory: stable, type: explicit)*
- **LRMs reason inconsistently across structurally similar puzzle types** — performance is dramatically better on tasks heavily represented in training data, with no evidence of transfer to novel compositional structures. *(severity: significant, trajectory: unclear, type: implicit_controlled_conditions)*

## Bottlenecks

- **Combinatorial explosion in ARC transformation search** — the space of transformations is near-infinite even with strong priors, making symbolic/search-based approaches computationally intractable at scale. Blocking: *symbolic and program synthesis approaches achieving broad ARC coverage without exponential compute scaling*. *(status: active, horizon: 3-5 years)*
- **Verification bottleneck in judgment-dependent domains** — the inability to verify correctness in subjective, qualitative domains blocks AI autonomy from expanding beyond narrow, objectively scorable tasks. Blocking: *full automation of knowledge work tasks in legal, creative, strategic, and other qualitative domains*. *(status: active, horizon: 3-5 years)*
- **LRMs cannot reliably execute exact sequential algorithmic computation** — even when given the algorithm explicitly, models fail to follow it step-by-step to correct conclusions. Blocking: *reliable use of LRMs for procedural execution tasks, formal verification, and exact computation in agentic pipelines*. *(status: active, horizon: 3-5 years)*

## Breakthroughs

- **Test-time training closes the neural-symbolic gap on ARC** — TTT pushed neural LLM coverage of program-synthesis-solvable tasks from 42.2% to 73.5%, the largest single-intervention gain recorded on ARC to date.
- **Controlled puzzle environments isolate planning from pattern-matching** — Tower of Hanoi / River Crossing divergence experiments provided the first clean empirical separation between trained-pattern retrieval and generalizable algorithmic execution in LRMs.
- **AIRV augmentation-and-vote achieves 260% accuracy boost** — demonstrating that inference-time augmentation is a first-class reasoning strategy, not merely a training-time technique.
- **Latent thought-augmented pretraining improves mathematical reasoning** — training on data with explicit reasoning traces during pretraining produces stronger mathematical reasoning without task-specific fine-tuning, implicating training data structure (not just scale) as a driver of reasoning capability.
- **SWE-bench SOTA achieved without custom model-specific scaffold** — signalling that general reasoning capability is becoming sufficient for repository-level software engineering, shifting the bottleneck from model capability to task grounding and long-horizon state management.

## Anticipations

- If LRMs remain impervious to explicit algorithmic guidance, reliable agentic pipelines requiring procedural execution will require fundamentally different architectures — hybrid systems or neuro-symbolic approaches — rather than LRM scaling.
- The synthetic data implication (strong CoT bootstraps richer pretraining data) is expected to reframe where reasoning capability investment concentrates: upstream data structure rather than post-training RLHF/RLAIF methods.
- The verification bottleneck is anticipated to become the dominant constraint on AI autonomy expansion in qualitative knowledge work domains within the 3-5 year horizon, absent a new approach to preference learning or judgment elicitation.
- ARC performance will likely continue improving incrementally through TTT and inference-time augmentation, but the ~36-point human gap is not expected to close with current approaches — a breakthrough in perceptual generalisation or compositional transfer is required.

## Contradictions

- **Benchmark progress vs. generalisation failure**: LRMs show consistent improvement on abstract reasoning benchmarks (ARC, GPQA, HLE) yet collapse on structurally similar out-of-distribution tasks. The same models that solve Tower of Hanoi N=10 cannot apply the solution algorithm to River Crossing — progress on metrics does not track progress on the underlying capability.
- **Scale vs. algorithmic execution**: The "algorithm provision doesn't help" finding contradicts naive scaling intuitions. Larger, more capable models are not better at executing explicit algorithms step-by-step; the failure mode appears qualitative rather than quantitative.
- **CoT as reasoning vs. CoT as reliability primitive**: Chain-of-thought is simultaneously framed as a reasoning capability (improves answer quality) and as a reliability engineering primitive for agent systems (prevents error cascades). These framings have different implications for when and how to elicit CoT — the reliability framing suggests it should be mandatory in agent loops even when it doesn't improve single-step accuracy.
- **Perceptual vs. symbolic ARC**: The finding that ARC is fundamentally perceptual/qualitative contradicts approaches (program synthesis, DSL search) that treat it as a symbolic inference problem. Both have achieved partial success, suggesting ARC conflates two distinct problem types.

## Research Opportunities

- **Interventions that break the "algorithm provision doesn't help" ceiling** — this is the field's highest-priority near-term empirical question. Approaches worth investigating: fine-tuning on explicit algorithm execution traces, structured scratchpad formats that enforce step-by-step computation, hybrid systems that offload sequential computation to a symbolic executor while LRM handles planning.
- **Perceptual generalisation as a distinct research axis** — given that ARC is fundamentally perceptual, treating abstract reasoning and symbolic inference as a single problem is likely counterproductive. A research agenda that separately attacks visual/perceptual generalisation (few-shot concept learning, transformation invariance) and symbolic chain-of-thought could make faster progress than unified approaches.
- **Augmentation-and-vote as a systematic inference strategy** — AIRV's 260% accuracy boost suggests this is underexplored. Principled study of augmentation strategies, ensemble diversity, and vote aggregation for reasoning tasks could yield gains comparable to architecture improvements.
- **Pretraining data structure for reasoning** — the latent thought-augmented pretraining result suggests that the composition of pretraining data (not just scale) is a first-class variable for reasoning capability. Systematic study of reasoning trace density, trace quality, and domain coverage in pretraining corpora is a tractable near-term research direction.
- **Verification methods for qualitative judgment** — the verification bottleneck in subjective domains is the dominant constraint on AI autonomy expansion. Research into preference learning from weak signals, constitutional approaches, and calibrated uncertainty estimation for qualitative tasks directly addresses this.
- **SE agent benchmarks as reasoning proxies** — if software engineering capability generalises to broader multi-step problem-solving, SE benchmarks may be the most practically grounded proxies for general reasoning. Designing SE benchmark variants that isolate reasoning from domain knowledge (e.g., synthetic unfamiliar codebases) could yield better reasoning evaluation.
